# Reflection: Ask Real-World Questions

## Topic
**Data conversation with a family member who manages the household budget**

I spoke with a family member who manages our household budget. They use data to track spending, manage bills, and make better financial decisions for the family.

---

## Question 1: What SMART questions did you ask? How did these questions tie into the field of the person you chatted with?

I asked questions such as: How do you track monthly spending? Which expenses are the highest each month? How have bills changed over the past year? These questions fit well because managing a household budget depends on clear, measurable, and time-based financial data.

---

## Question 2: What insights did you discover during your conversation?

I learned that they use bank statements and a budgeting app to keep track of expenses. One important insight was that grocery and utility costs increased the most over the last year. This showed that reviewing spending data can help them adjust the family budget more carefully.

---

## Question 3: How did the SMART framework help you arrive at your conclusions?

The SMART framework helped me ask questions that were clear, focused, and tied to a specific time period. Instead of getting general answers, I learned exactly which expenses changed and how those changes affected budgeting decisions.

---

## Key Takeaway

Using SMART questions made the conversation more organized and useful. It helped turn a general discussion about household spending into a focused data conversation with practical insights.
