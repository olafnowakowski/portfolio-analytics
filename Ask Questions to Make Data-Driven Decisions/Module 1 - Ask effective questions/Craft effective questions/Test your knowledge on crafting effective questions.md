### A data analyst uses the SMART methodology to create a question that encourages change. In the SMART methodology, how is this question type described? 
- In the SMART methodology, questions that encourage change are described as action-oriented.

### When formulating a Specific (S) question under the SMART methodology, which of the following details are prioritized to ensure the scope of the analysis is narrow and focused? Select all that apply.
- Focusing on a single topic or a few closely related ideas.
- Ensuring the language used is simple and unambiguous.
- Defining the key subject and metric clearly.

### A data analyst working for a coffee roaster is writing questions for a customer experience survey. One of the questions asks, “Do you prefer online or in-store?” Then, they rewrite it to ask, “Do you prefer purchasing coffee beans through our online marketplace or by shopping at our retail store?” Why is this a more effective question?
- The first question is vague, whereas the second question includes important context.
