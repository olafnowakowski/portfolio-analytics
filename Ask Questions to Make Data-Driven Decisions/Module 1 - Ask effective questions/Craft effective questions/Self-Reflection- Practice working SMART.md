## Scenario

You are a **junior data analyst**.  
Your company has collected **weekend sales data**, and your manager asks you to perform a **thorough exploration** of it.

Before beginning the analysis, you need to ask the right questions to understand:

- the goal of the project
- who needs the results
- when the project is due
- what data and tools are available
- who can access the data

---

## SMART Questions Reminder

SMART questions should be:

- **Specific**: focused and clear
- **Measurable**: answerable with data
- **Action-oriented**: useful for decision-making
- **Relevant**: connected to the business problem
- **Time-bound**: linked to a clear time period

---

## Main Topics to Ask About

### 1. Objectives

These questions help define **what the project is trying to achieve**.

**Examples:**

- What is the main goal of exploring the weekend sales data?
- Are we looking for sales trends, best-selling products, or customer behavior?
- What business decision should this analysis support?

**Why it matters:**  
Without clear objectives, the analysis may become too broad or unfocused.

---

### 2. Audience

These questions help identify **who will use the results**.

**Examples:**

- Who are the stakeholders for this project?
- Who will I present the results to?
- What level of detail does the audience need?

**Why it matters:**  
Different audiences need different types of reports. A manager may want a summary, while an analyst may want detailed numbers.

---

### 3. Time

These questions define **the deadline and the time period being studied**.

**Examples:**

- When is the project due?
- Which weekends are included in the data?
- Are we analyzing one weekend, one month, or a longer period?

**Why it matters:**  
Time-bound questions help you focus on the correct dataset and manage your work schedule.

---

### 4. Resources

These questions help determine **what tools, data, and support are available**.

**Examples:**

- What datasets are available for the analysis?
- Do I have access to sales dashboards or past reports?
- Are there team members who can help explain the data?

**Why it matters:**  
Knowing your resources helps you choose the right method and avoid delays.

---

### 5. Security

These questions clarify **who can access the data and how it should be handled**.

**Examples:**

- Is there any sensitive customer or sales information in the dataset?
- Who is allowed to access this data?
- Are there any privacy or company rules I need to follow?

**Why it matters:**  
Security questions protect data and ensure the project follows company policies.

---

## Most Important Questions to Ask First

At the start of the project, the most important questions are:

- What is the goal of this analysis?
- Who are the stakeholders?
- What does success look like?
- What time period does the dataset cover?
- When is the project due?
- What resources and tools are available?
- Are there any data privacy or access restrictions?
