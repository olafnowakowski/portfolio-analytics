# Ask
Nikki and her team began by identifying the core problem: how to tell whether Nooglers were onboarding faster through the new onboarding program compared to the old lecture-based program. They asked many questions and worked closely with content providers to define what “onboarding faster” actually meant.

# Prepare
The team prepared the data by identifying the population of new hires they wanted to study. They determined the sample set, control group, and experiment group. They also gathered the relevant data sources and made sure the data was in a clean, usable format for writing scripts.

# Process
Next, they processed the data so it could be analyzed effectively. This included organizing it into the correct format, columns, and tables, making it ready for analysis in SQL.

# Analyze
To analyze the data, the team wrote scripts in SQL and R. They compared the control group with the experiment group and looked for differences in behavioral indicators. This helped them determine whether the new onboarding program was more effective.

# Share
After completing the analysis, they shared the results with stakeholders in ways that were easy to understand. Depending on the audience, they created reports, dashboards, and presentations to communicate their findings clearly.

# Act
The results were positive, so the team decided to continue using the project-based learning onboarding program. The data showed that the program worked: new hires learned better, became productive faster, and returned to their jobs more quickly.
