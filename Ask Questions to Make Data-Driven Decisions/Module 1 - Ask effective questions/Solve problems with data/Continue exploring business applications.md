# Six Common Problem Types in Data Analytics

Data analysts solve many different kinds of problems. A useful first step is to identify **what type of problem** you are dealing with. Once you know the problem type, it becomes easier to choose the right data, tools, and analysis method.

Below are six common problem types, along with clear examples.

---

## 1. Making Predictions

**Definition:**  
Using data to make an informed guess about what is likely to happen in the future.

**Example:**  
Anywhere Gaming Repair wanted to know the best advertising method to attract new customers. Analysts used past data to estimate which advertising option would most likely work best for their target audience.

**Key idea:**  
Predictions do not guarantee the future, but they help businesses make smarter decisions.

---

## 2. Categorizing Things

**Definition:**  
Sorting information into groups based on shared features or characteristics.

**Example:**  
A business wants to improve customer satisfaction. Analysts review customer service calls and group key words or phrases into categories such as:

- politeness
- satisfaction
- dissatisfaction
- empathy

This helps the company identify strong performers and employees who may need more coaching.

**Key idea:**  
Categorizing makes large amounts of data easier to organize and understand.

---

## 3. Spotting Something Unusual

**Definition:**  
Finding data that is different from the normal pattern.

**Example:**  
A smartwatch tracks a user's health data, such as heart rate. If the user’s normal resting heart rate is around 70 beats per minute, but it suddenly rises to 120, the device detects something unusual and sends an alert.

**Key idea:**  
Spotting unusual data can help detect problems early and even prevent serious outcomes.

---

## 4. Identifying Themes

**Definition:**  
Grouping information into broader ideas or major topics.

**Example:**  
A user experience designer wants to improve a coffee maker. Survey data from users is analyzed to identify common themes in customer feedback. One major theme shows that users often cannot tell whether the coffee maker is on or off.

This insight leads to a design improvement, such as better lighting or placement of the power button.

**Key idea:**  
Identifying themes helps analysts find the bigger meaning in feedback and behavior.

### Difference Between Categorizing Things and Identifying Themes

- **Categorizing things** means putting data into specific groups.
- **Identifying themes** means combining those groups into broader ideas.

**Example:**

- **Category:** confusion about button placement
- **Theme:** poor usability

---

## 5. Discovering Connections

**Definition:**  
Finding relationships between different factors, systems, or organizations.

**Example:**  
A third-party logistics company wants to reduce truck wait times. By sharing data with partner companies, analysts discover that scheduling differences are causing delays. For example, one company delivers on Mondays, Wednesdays, and Fridays, while the other only handles shipments on Tuesdays and Thursdays.

Once the connection is discovered, both companies can adjust schedules to reduce delays.

**Key idea:**  
Discovering connections helps reveal root causes and improve coordination.

---

## 6. Finding Patterns

**Definition:**  
Looking at historical data to find repeated trends or behaviors.

**Example:**  
An oil and gas company wants to prevent machine breakdowns. Analysts review maintenance records and discover a pattern: machines begin failing more often when maintenance is delayed beyond a 15-day cycle.

This pattern helps the company schedule maintenance more effectively and reduce downtime.

**Key idea:**  
Patterns in past data can help organizations prevent future problems.

---

## Quick Review Table

| Problem Type | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Making predictions | Estimating what may happen in the future | Choosing the best advertising method |
| Categorizing things | Grouping data by shared features | Sorting customer service call language |
| Spotting something unusual | Detecting abnormal data | Smartwatch alert for unusual heart rate |
| Identifying themes | Finding broader ideas in data | Coffee maker feedback showing usability issues |
| Discovering connections | Finding relationships between causes and effects | Shared shipping data revealing scheduling delays |
| Finding patterns | Identifying repeated trends in past data | Machine failures linked to delayed maintenance |

---

## Key Takeaway

The six common problem types in data analytics are:

1. **Making predictions**
2. **Categorizing things**
3. **Spotting something unusual**
4. **Identifying themes**
5. **Discovering connections**
6. **Finding patterns**

Understanding the problem type is an important first step in data analysis because it helps analysts focus on the right questions, choose the right data, and produce useful solutions.
