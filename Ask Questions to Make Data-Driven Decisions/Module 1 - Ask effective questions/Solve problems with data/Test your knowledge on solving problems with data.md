# Streaming Service Scenario: Understanding Why Customers Cancel

## 1. Best Starting Problem Type: Spotting Something Unusual

The best starting point is **spotting something unusual**. This helps the data professional compare customers who cancel with customers who stay subscribed and identify behaviors that stand out. For example, the analyst might notice that customers who cancel often show unusual changes such as a sudden drop in watch time, repeated app errors, more payment failures, or fewer logins in the weeks before canceling.

This is a strong first step because it helps the business quickly find warning signs that are different from normal customer behavior. Once those unusual behaviors are identified, the company can investigate them more deeply.

---

## 2. How Identifying Themes Extends the Analysis

After spotting unusual behaviors, the analyst can use **identifying themes** to group similar issues into broader ideas that are easier for the business to understand and act on.

For example:

- slow streaming, app crashes, and login problems could form the theme **technical issues**
- high price, billing complaints, and canceled renewals after a price change could form the theme **cost concerns**
- low watch time, lack of new shows, and repeated searches with no viewing could form the theme **content dissatisfaction**

This extends the analysis because it moves from individual signals to bigger business insights. Instead of seeing many separate complaints, the streaming service can understand the main reasons behind cancellations and decide which area needs the most attention.

---

## 3. Example of Using Finding Patterns

The analyst can use **finding patterns** to discover repeated trends in customer data over time.

For example, they might find a pattern showing that cancellations often happen:

- within one month after a subscription price increase
- right after a free trial ends
- after two or three weeks of very low viewing activity
- after repeated buffering or playback problems

This gives the business useful insight into when customers are most likely to leave and what events usually happen before cancellation.

---

## 4. How Making Predictions Helps the Business Make a Data-Driven Decision

The analyst can use **making predictions** to estimate which customers are most likely to cancel soon. For example, if a customer’s watch time drops sharply, they experience several app issues, and they recently viewed the account cancellation page, the model could predict that this customer has a high risk of churning.

This helps the business make data-driven decisions. The streaming service could:

- offer a discount before renewal
- recommend more relevant content
- send a retention email
- fix service issues for high-risk users first

In this way, predictions help the company take action early instead of waiting until the customer has already canceled.
