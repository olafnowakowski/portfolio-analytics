# 📊 Stakeholder Expectations in Data Analysis

## 🔑 What is a Stakeholder?
- A **stakeholder** is anyone who:
  - Has invested **time, interest, or resources** in a project
  - Is affected by the outcome of the project
- They depend on your analysis to **support their own decisions and tasks**
- In simple terms: they have a **“stake” in your work**

---

## 🎯 Why Stakeholder Expectations Are Important
- Your analysis must **match stakeholder needs and goals**
- Understanding expectations helps you:
  - Stay **focused on the right problem**
  - Deliver **useful and relevant insights**
- If expectations are not met:
  - Your work may be **ignored or misunderstood**

---

## 🗣️ Communication with Stakeholders
- Clear and regular communication is **essential**
- Stakeholders typically want to discuss:
  - **Project objectives** (what problem to solve)
  - **Requirements** (data, tools, resources needed)
  - **Challenges or risks**
- Benefits of communication:
  - Builds **trust and confidence**
  - Prevents **misalignment**
  - Keeps the project **moving smoothly**

---

## 🧩 Example: HR Turnover Project

### 📉 Problem
- Company notices an **increase in employee turnover rate**
  - *Turnover rate = how often employees leave the company*
- HR team wants:
  - To understand **why employees are leaving**
  - To find **solutions to reduce turnover**

---

## 👥 Key Stakeholders and Roles

### 🧑‍💼 VP of HR (Executive Stakeholder)
- Focus:
  - **Big-picture insights**
  - Patterns among employees who leave
  - Relationship between:
    - Employee **engagement**
    - Employee **productivity**
- Role:
  - Makes **strategic decisions**
- Note:
  - May not be involved in **day-to-day communication**

---

### 📋 Project Manager
- Responsible for:
  - **Planning and executing** the project
  - Tracking **progress and deadlines**
- Your responsibilities:
  - Provide **regular updates**
  - Communicate:
    - What you **need**
    - Any **issues or blockers**
- Acts as a **bridge** between you and higher-level stakeholders

---

### 🧾 HR Administrators
- Need:
  - Clear understanding of **metrics** used in analysis
- Purpose:
  - To collect **accurate and relevant employee data**
- Your role:
  - Ensure metrics are **clear and usable**

---

### 📊 Other Data Analysts
- May analyze:
  - Different datasets (e.g., hiring data)
- Important to:
  - **Share insights**
  - Combine findings for a **complete analysis**

---

## 🔍 Key Findings from Analysis
- Data shows:
  - Drop in **engagement and performance** after **13 months**
- Interpretation:
  - Employees may feel:
    - **Disconnected**
    - **Unmotivated**
  - This leads to **higher chance of leaving**

---

## 🔗 Combining Team Insights
- Another analyst discovers:
  - A **large hiring increase** ~18 months ago
- By sharing findings:
  - Team gains a **broader understanding**
  - Insights become more **actionable**

---

## ✅ Final Decision & Impact
- VP decides to:
  - Introduce **manager check-ins at 12 months**
  - Focus on **career development discussions**
- Outcome:
  - Employees feel more **supported and engaged**
  - **Turnover decreases** after the 13-month mark

---

## 🤝 Key Takeaways
- Always identify:
  - **Who your stakeholders are**
  - **What they expect from you**
- Tailor communication based on:
  - Their **role**
  - Their **level of involvement**
- Good stakeholder management leads to:
  - Better **project outcomes**
  - Stronger **team collaboration**
  - Increased **trust in your analysis**

---

## 🧠 Quick Summary
- Stakeholders = people who **care about and rely on your work**
- Your job:
  - Understand their needs
  - Communicate clearly
  - Deliver relevant insights
- Success comes from:
  - **Collaboration + communication + alignment**
