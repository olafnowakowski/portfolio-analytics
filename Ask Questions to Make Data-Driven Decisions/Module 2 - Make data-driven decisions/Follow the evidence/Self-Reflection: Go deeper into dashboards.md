# Dashboard Reflection Notes

## Activity Overview

A **dashboard** is a single point of access for managing business information. It helps analysts pull key information from data and present it visually so findings are easier to understand.

Businesses often create dashboards for different purposes. The three most common types are:

- **Strategic dashboards**
- **Operational dashboards**
- **Analytical dashboards**

---

## Types of Dashboards

### Strategic Dashboards
Strategic dashboards focus on **long-term goals** and **high-level metrics**.  
They are usually used for enterprise-wide decision-making and often cover longer time periods, such as a quarter or a year.

### Operational Dashboards
Operational dashboards focus on **short-term performance tracking** and **intermediate goals**.  
They often show data from days, weeks, or months and help businesses monitor daily operations in near real time.

### Analytical Dashboards
Analytical dashboards contain large amounts of detailed data and are often used by **data analysts** or **data science teams**.  
They are more technical and focus on deep analysis, predictions, and detailed financial or performance metrics.

---

## Question 1: How are the different types of dashboards similar to each other?

All three types of dashboards are used to **visualize data** and make information easier to understand. They also help businesses track important **metrics** and support better decision-making by showing performance clearly.

---

## Question 2: In what ways do they differ?

The main difference is their **purpose** and **time frame**. Strategic dashboards focus on long-term goals, operational dashboards track short-term or daily performance, and analytical dashboards focus on detailed datasets and technical analysis.

---

## Helpful Feedback Notes for Question 1

### Similarities
- all dashboards are **visual tools**
- all dashboards help users understand data more clearly
- all dashboards use **metrics** to evaluate performance

### Differences
- **strategic dashboards** usually show a longer time frame, such as a quarter or a year
- **operational dashboards** usually show shorter time frames, such as days or weeks
- **analytical dashboards** focus more on detailed KPIs, datasets, and technical analysis than on a specific time frame

---

## Question 3: What is an example of a data source a company might use with a dashboard?

A company might use **call center data** as a source for a dashboard. For example, it could track call length, number of calls received, number of resolved calls, and calls received during specific date ranges.

---

## Question 4: How would a company benefit from a dashboard that uses this data?

A dashboard could help the company identify patterns such as the busiest days or months, average call times, and service performance. This would make it easier to plan staffing needs, improve customer service, and respond more quickly to operational issues.

---

## Question 5: What industries or businesses might benefit from using dashboards more than others?

Industries such as **call centers**, **finance**, **sales**, **marketing**, and **technology** can benefit greatly from dashboards. Larger companies and businesses in fast-changing markets often gain the most value because dashboards help them monitor performance and make quicker data-informed decisions.

---

## Helpful Feedback Notes for Question 2

Dashboards can help companies:

- track historical and current performance
- set short-term and long-term goals
- define key performance indicators (KPIs)
- identify inefficiencies or problem areas
- make faster and more informed decisions

### Businesses that benefit most
- larger companies
- companies with many products or services
- businesses in fast-changing industries like:
  - marketing
  - sales
  - technology
  - finance
  - customer service

---

## Key Takeaway

Dashboards are powerful tools that help businesses organize, visualize, and monitor data. While all dashboards support decision-making, each type serves a different purpose depending on whether the focus is long-term strategy, short-term operations, or detailed analysis.
