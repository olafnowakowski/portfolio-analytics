# Quantitative and Qualitative Data Notes

## Introduction

Data is a collection of facts, and data analysis helps reveal patterns and insights that support better decision-making. Different questions produce different kinds of data, and two major types are:

- **Quantitative data**
- **Qualitative data**

Understanding the difference between them helps data analysts ask better questions and solve problems more effectively.

---

## Quantitative Data

**Quantitative data** focuses on specific, objective, and numerical facts.

It usually answers questions like:

- **what**
- **how many**
- **how often**

### Examples
- how many commuters take the train each week
- average customer rating
- number of negative reviews
- total sales this month

### Key idea
Quantitative data is measurable and can often be shown in:

- charts
- graphs
- tables

---

## Qualitative Data

**Qualitative data** focuses on qualities, characteristics, descriptions, and explanations.

It usually helps answer **why** questions.

### Examples
- why customers prefer one product over another
- why people are unhappy with a service
- hair color
- customer comments in reviews

### Key idea
Qualitative data gives context and deeper meaning to the numbers.

---

## Why Both Types Matter

As a data analyst, you often need **both quantitative and qualitative data**.

- **Quantitative data** shows what is happening
- **Qualitative data** helps explain why it is happening

Using both types together gives a fuller understanding of a problem.

---

## Example: Customer Reviews

Customer reviews are a good example of both data types working together.

### Quantitative questions
These questions produce measurable results:

- How many negative reviews are there?
- What is the average rating?
- How many reviews use the same keyword?

These answers confirm whether customers are satisfied or dissatisfied.

### Qualitative questions
These questions help explain the situation:

- Why are customers unhappy?
- How can we improve their experience?

These answers provide the reasons behind the numbers.

---

## Ice Cream Shop Example

A local ice cream shop notices that its rating has been going down. The owner wants to understand why.

### Step 1: Use quantitative data
The owner asks measurable questions such as:

- How many negative reviews are there?
- What is the average rating?
- How many reviews use the same keywords?

The owner notices that **17 negative reviews** use the word **"frustrated."**

This is **quantitative data** because it is a count.

### Step 2: Use qualitative data
Next, the owner asks:

- Why are customers using the word "frustrated"?

After reading the reviews more carefully, the owner discovers that customers are frustrated because the shop runs out of popular flavors before the end of the day.

This is **qualitative data** because it explains the reason behind the repeated complaint.

### Step 3: Make a decision
With both types of data, the owner decides to change the shop’s weekly order so there will be enough popular flavors available.

---

## Main Lesson from the Ice Cream Shop

The shop owner was able to:

- identify that customers were unhappy using **quantitative data**
- understand why they were unhappy using **qualitative data**
- make better business decisions using both together

---

## Role of the Data Analyst

A data analyst needs to know:

- which questions to ask
- when to use quantitative data
- when to use qualitative data
- how to combine both types to find the best solution

This is why analysts are sometimes called **data detectives**.

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Quantitative data** is numerical and measurable.
- **Qualitative data** is descriptive and explanatory.
- Quantitative data often answers **what**, **how many**, and **how often**.
- Qualitative data often answers **why**.
- Both types of data are important for strong analysis and better decision-making.
- Using both together helps businesses understand problems and choose the right actions.

---

## Quick Comparison Table

| Data Type | Meaning | Answers | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| Quantitative | Numerical, measurable data | What, how many, how often | 17 reviews used the word "frustrated" |
| Qualitative | Descriptive, explanatory data | Why, how, what kind | Customers were frustrated because flavors sold out early |

---

## Short Summary

Quantitative and qualitative data are both important in data analysis. Quantitative data provides measurable facts, while qualitative data explains the reasons behind those facts. Together, they help analysts understand problems more clearly and make better decisions.
