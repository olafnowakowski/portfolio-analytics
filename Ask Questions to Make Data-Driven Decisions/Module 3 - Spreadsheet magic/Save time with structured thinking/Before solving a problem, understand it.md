# Structured Thinking and Problem Domain Notes

## Main Idea

Before solving a problem, a data analyst must first **define the problem clearly**.  
If the problem is not understood correctly, the team may:

- solve the wrong problem
- collect the wrong data
- waste time, money, and resources

---

## Key Quote

Albert Einstein said:

*"If I were given one hour to save the planet, I would spend 59 minutes defining the problem and one minute resolving it."*

### Meaning
This quote shows that understanding the problem is often the most important part of solving it.

---

## What Is the Problem Domain?

The **problem domain** is the specific area of analysis that includes:

- every activity affecting the problem
- every activity affected by the problem
- all related parts and relationships

### Why it matters
Understanding the problem domain helps analysts see the full picture before starting analysis.

---

## Why Defining the Problem Matters

If the problem is clearly defined from the start:

- the solution is easier to find
- the right data can be collected
- the analysis is more efficient
- the team avoids wasted effort

---
