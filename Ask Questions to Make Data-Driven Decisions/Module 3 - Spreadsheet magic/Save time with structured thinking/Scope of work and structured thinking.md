# Structured Thinking and Scope of Work Notes

## Main Idea

Carefully defining a business problem can save:

- time
- money
- resources

This is achieved through **structured thinking**.

---

## What Is Structured Thinking?

**Structured thinking** is the process of:

- recognizing the current problem or situation
- organizing available information
- revealing gaps and opportunities
- identifying the options

### Simple meaning
Structured thinking means being well prepared before starting the analysis.

It includes:

- a clear understanding of the problem
- a list of expected deliverables
- a timeline for major tasks
- checkpoints to measure progress

---

## Why Structured Thinking Matters

In business, teams sometimes spend many hours trying to solve a problem but end up back where they started.

### Why this happens
This often happens because the team did not fully understand the issue from the beginning.

### How structured thinking helps
Structured thinking helps analysts:

- understand problems at a high level
- identify areas that need deeper investigation
- avoid wasted effort
- make the work easier and more organized

---

## Starting Point: Problem Domain

The first step in structured thinking is understanding the **problem domain**.

### Problem domain
The **problem domain** is the specific area of analysis connected to the problem.

Once you understand the problem domain, you can:

- set a strong foundation
- define requirements
- form hypotheses
- prepare for investigation

---

## Example of a Missing Variable

Structured thinking helps prevent mistakes caused by missing information.

### Example
Suppose you are asked to predict the future value of an apartment building using a dataset.

You may have hundreds of variables, and all of them may matter.

If one important variable is missing, such as:

- square footage

then your conclusions may be wrong and you may have to redo the analysis.

### Key lesson
Missing variables can lead to inaccurate conclusions.

---

## Scope of Work (SOW)

One important tool for structured thinking is a **scope of work**.

### Definition
A **scope of work (SOW)** is an agreed-upon outline of the work to be performed on a project.

### Reflect

Be sure to differentiate statement of work from scope of work, which are both abbreviated as SOW. Although they help define deliverables and a timeline, they aren't the same and shouldn't be used interchangeably.
A statement of work is a document that clearly identifies the products and services a vendor or contractor will provide to an organization. It includes objectives, guidelines, deliverables, schedule, and costs. 
A scope of work is project-based and sets the expectations and boundaries of a project. A scope of work may be included in a statement of work to help define project outcomes. 
> _As a junior data analyst, it's more typical to be asked to create a scope of work than a statement of work._

---

## What a Scope of Work Usually Includes

A scope of work often includes:

- work details
- schedules
- reports
- deliverables
- milestones

For data analysts, it may also include:

- data preparation
- data validation
- quantitative analysis
- qualitative analysis
- initial results
- visualizations

---

## Why a Scope of Work Is Useful

A strong scope of work helps:

- reduce confusion
- address contradictions early
- answer questions up front
- keep the project organized
- prevent delays and setbacks

---

## Wedding Planner Example

The lesson uses a wedding planner example to explain a scope of work.

### Focus task
Wedding invitations

### Example deliverables
- decide on invitation design
- make the guest list
- collect addresses
- print invitations
- address envelopes
- add stamps
- mail invitations

### Other parts of the scope
- **timeline:** dates for each task
- **milestones:** checkpoints to stay on track
- **reports:** updates that show what has been completed

### Meaning
Just like a wedding project needs clear planning, a data analysis project also needs a clear structure.

---

## How Structured Thinking Helps Data Analysts

Structured thinking allows analysts to:

- understand the work more clearly
- prepare for obstacles
- organize tasks logically
- reduce mistakes
- improve project outcomes

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Structured thinking** is a method for organizing and solving problems clearly.
- It begins with understanding the **problem domain**.
- It helps reveal missing information, gaps, and opportunities.
- A **scope of work** is a useful tool for planning project tasks and expectations.
- Missing variables or unclear requirements can lead to wasted effort and inaccurate conclusions.
- Structured thinking makes data analysis more efficient, organized, and reliable.

---

## Short Summary

Structured thinking helps data analysts clearly define problems, organize information, and identify the best path forward before starting analysis. A **scope of work** supports this process by outlining deliverables, timelines, milestones, and reports. Together, these tools help prevent confusion, reduce mistakes, and improve the quality of analysis.
