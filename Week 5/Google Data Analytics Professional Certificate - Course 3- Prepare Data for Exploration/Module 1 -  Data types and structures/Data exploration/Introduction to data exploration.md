# Introduction to Preparing Data

## Main Idea

After learning how to ask questions, define problems, work with stakeholders, and communicate insights, the next major step is learning how to **prepare data correctly**.

Preparing data is important because the quality, type, structure, and credibility of the data affect the quality of the analysis.

---

# Why Preparing Data Matters

Even if you ask the right questions and understand the business problem, your analysis will only be useful if the data is prepared properly.

Preparing data helps you:

- choose the right data for the question
- understand how data is generated and collected
- organize data correctly
- protect data
- check whether data is valid and relevant
- identify possible bias
- use data to tell a strong and accurate story

### Important Lesson

Good analysis depends on good data preparation.

---

# The Data Analysis Process

You have already learned the six main steps of the data analysis process:

1. **Ask**
2. **Prepare**
3. **Process**
4. **Analyze**
5. **Share**
6. **Act**

This course focuses mainly on the **Prepare** step.

---

# What the Prepare Step Means

The **Prepare** step is about getting the right data ready for analysis.

This includes understanding:

- what data you need
- where the data comes from
- how the data was collected
- whether the data is reliable
- whether the data is biased
- whether the data is clean
- how the data is structured
- how to access and protect the data

---

# Why Data Types and Data Structures Matter

Different business questions require different types of data.

Understanding data types and data structures helps you decide:

- what data is most useful
- what format the data should be in
- how to organize the data
- how to extract the data
- how to use the data correctly

### Important Lesson

The type and structure of data should match the question you are trying to answer.

---

# Data as a Story

Data can be the main character in a powerful story.

A data analyst uses data to explain:

- what is happening
- why it matters
- what patterns exist
- what decisions should be made
- what actions should be taken

The goal is not only to analyze data, but to tell a clear and useful story with it.

---

# Example: Healthcare and Medicare Data

Hallie, an analytical lead at Google, works with healthcare companies.

Her work involves using analytics to help healthcare companies create digital marketing solutions that strengthen their business and brands.

One example she gives is analyzing **Medicare enrollment data over time**.

She connects this data to how people research Medicare plans on Google.

---

## What She Tries to Understand

She studies whether:

- Medicare enrollments are increasing
- people aged 65 and older are becoming more informed healthcare decision-makers
- Google searches play a role in increased demand for Medicare plans

---

## Important Data Concerns in This Example

When working with healthcare and search data, she must make sure the data is:

- relevant
- valid
- ethical to use
- respectful of privacy
- connected to questions of access and equity

### Important Lesson

Data analysts must not only find useful data. They must also make sure the data is appropriate, valid, and responsibly handled.

---

# Privacy, Access, and Equity

Data analysis can affect real people.

In the healthcare example, it is important to protect the privacy of people searching for Medicare information.

It is also important to think about:

- who has access to healthcare information
- whether the data represents different groups fairly
- whether the analysis supports equitable outcomes

### Important Lesson

Responsible data analysis includes protecting privacy and thinking about fairness.
