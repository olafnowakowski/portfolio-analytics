# How Data Is Generated and Collected

## Main Idea

Data is being generated constantly all around the world.

Every day, people create huge amounts of data through:

- texts
- emails
- online searches
- video views
- social media activity
- mobile device use
- digital photos
- surveys
- interviews
- forms
- scientific observations
- website activity

Understanding how data is generated helps Data Analysts understand the context behind the data they use.

---

# What Is Data?

Every piece of information is data.

Data can come from many different activities, both online and offline.

Examples of data include:

- a text message
- an email
- a search query
- a video view
- a digital photo
- a survey response
- an interview answer
- a website click
- a form submission
- an observation in a scientific study

---

# Data Is Generated Through Activity

Most data is created as a result of people’s activity in the world.

Because people spend a lot of time online, large amounts of data are created through digital activity.

Examples of online activity that generates data:

- posting on social media
- watching videos
- searching online
- sending messages
- clicking links
- using mobile apps
- uploading photos

---

# Digital Photos as Data

A digital photo is one piece of data, but it also contains more data inside it.

A photo may include data about:

- number of pixels
- colors in each pixel
- image size
- file type
- time and date created
- device information

### Important Lesson

Even simple digital objects, like photos, can contain many layers of data.

---

# Data Can Be Generated by Collecting Information

Data is not only created automatically through digital activity.

It can also be generated intentionally by collecting information.

Common ways to collect and generate data include:

- forms
- questionnaires
- surveys
- interviews
- observations
- experiments
- customer feedback

---

# Ethics, Rights, and Privacy

When data is generated or collected, it must be handled ethically.

Analysts and organizations need to protect people’s:

- rights
- privacy
- personal information
- consent
- safety

### Important Lesson

Collecting data is powerful, but it must be done responsibly.

---

# Example: United States Census Bureau

The **United States Census Bureau** collects data about the country’s population using forms.

This data can be used for important public decisions, such as funding for:

- schools
- hospitals
- fire departments
- public services

The Census Bureau also collects data about U.S. businesses.

---

## Why Census Data Is Useful

The data collected by the Census Bureau can be used by others for:

- analysis
- planning
- public funding decisions
- business decisions
- understanding population trends
- understanding business needs

### Important Lesson

Organizations can create data through collection, and that data can later be used by others for analysis.

---

# Example: Annual Business Survey

The **Annual Business Survey** helps identify the needs of businesses.

It can be used to understand:

- what businesses need
- how resources can help businesses succeed
- trends in business activity
- challenges businesses face

---

# Example: Healthcare Surveys

The speaker works in analytics for the healthcare industry.

Her team runs surveys to understand how patients feel about healthcare-related topics.

One example survey asked patients how they feel about:

- telemedicine
- in-person doctor visits

The data collected from these surveys helps healthcare companies improve patient care.

### Important Lesson

Survey data can help organizations understand people’s experiences and improve services.

---

# Surveys Are Only One Type of Data Collection

Survey data is just one example.

There are many ways to collect data depending on the question being asked.

Other methods include:

- interviews
- observations
- forms
- questionnaires
- website tracking
- scientific experiments

---

# Interviews as Data Collection

An interview is also a way to collect data.

## Example: Job Interview

In a job interview, you share information about yourself with the hiring manager.

That information becomes data.

The hiring manager collects and analyzes that data to decide whether to hire you.

---

## Data Collection Goes Both Ways

The job candidate can also collect data about the company.

This can help them decide:

- whether the company is a good fit
- what questions to ask
- whether the job matches their goals
- what the company culture might be like

### Important Lesson

Interviews are not only conversations; they are also exchanges of data.

---

# Scientific Data Generation

Scientists also generate data through observation and research.

Examples include:

- studying animal behavior
- observing bacteria under a microscope
- recording experiment results
- measuring natural events
- collecting environmental information

Scientific observations create data that can later be analyzed.

---

# Common Data Collection Tools

Common tools for collecting and generating data include:

- forms
- questionnaires
- surveys

These tools are used because they allow organizations to collect structured information from many people.

---

# Online Data and Cookies

### To track people's online activities and interests, which method of data collection is most effective?
- Cookies
> To track people's online activities and interests, cookies are most effective

Not all online data is generated directly or obviously.

Some data is collected in the background while people use websites.

One common method is through **cookies**.

---

# What Are Cookies?

**Cookies** are small files stored on computers that contain information about users.

Cookies can help websites remember:

- preferences
- browsing activity
- interests
- habits
- previous visits

---

# How Cookies Are Used

Cookies can help advertisers understand user interests and online habits.

For example, they can help explain why:

- online ads seem personalized
- websites remember preferences
- product suggestions seem accurate

Cookies can provide useful information without personally identifying the user.

### Important Lesson

Online behavior can generate data even when users are not directly filling out a form or survey.

---

# Why Data Generation Matters for Analysts

As a Data Analyst, you will work with many types of data.

Understanding how the data was generated helps you understand:

- where the data came from
- what the data means
- whether the data is useful
- whether the data is reliable
- what limitations the data may have
- what context is needed for analysis

---

# Why Data Collection Matters for Analysts

Knowing how to collect data helps make the data analysis process more efficient.

Good data collection helps ensure that the data is:

- relevant
- useful
- organized
- ethical
- appropriate for the question
- easier to analyze

---

# Big Lesson

Data is created constantly through human activity, digital systems, surveys, forms, interviews, scientific observations, and online behavior.

To analyze data well, a Data Analyst needs to understand both how data was generated and how it was collected.

---

# Key Takeaways

- Data is generated all around the world every second.
- Every piece of information is data.
- People generate data through digital activity like texts, emails, searches, video views, social media, and photos.
- Digital photos contain data such as pixels and colors.
- Data can also be generated through intentional collection methods like surveys, forms, questionnaires, and interviews.
- Data collection must be done ethically and with respect for rights and privacy.
- The U.S. Census Bureau collects population and business data using forms.
- Census data helps support public decisions like funding schools, hospitals, and fire departments.
- Healthcare surveys can help companies improve patient care.
- Interviews are also a form of data collection.
- Scientists generate data through observations and experiments.
- Cookies are small files stored on computers that collect information about online behavior.
- Cookies can help websites remember preferences and help advertisers understand interests.
- Understanding how data is generated gives important context for analysis.
- Knowing how to collect data makes analysis more efficient.

---

# Extra Important Notes

- Data can be created actively, such as when someone fills out a survey.
- Data can also be created passively, such as through cookies or website activity.
- Ethical data collection is important because data often comes from real people.
- Context matters: the way data is generated affects how it should be interpreted.
- Data Analysts should always ask where the data came from and how it was collected.
- More data does not automatically mean better analysis; the data must be relevant and responsibly collected.
