### Why is gathering business requirements important in a business process analysis?
- Gathering business requirements helps avoid situations where there could be a mismatch between what is being designed and what the actual needs of the business are.
> Gathering business requirements can confirm the validity of the problem and make sure the solution appropriately meets all the requirements.

### Which of the following accurately describes a technical requirement?
- A requirement that must be addressed in order to successfully implement the process or manufacture the product. 
> Technical requirements are challenges that must be addressed in order to successfully implement the process or manufacture the product.

### Which of the following accurately describes a functional requirement?
- A requirement that specifies how a product, service, or solution should work from the standpoint of the end user.
> Functional requirements specify how a product, service, or solution should work from the standpoint of the end user. They explain the features and functions with which the end-user will directly engage.

### Which of the following describes a benefit of using brainstorming as a way to elicit requirements?

### Which elicitation technique gives you the opportunity to go in depth with stakeholders but can be a time-consuming process?
- Interviews 
> Interviews allow you to go in depth with stakeholders, but they can take more time than some of the other elicitation techniques.

### Which elicitation technique gives you an opportunity to watch clients interact with the process or products to gain insights directly from user experience?
- Interface analysis
> During interface analysis, you will investigate how a client interacts with the product or process to gather insights into behavior and paint points.
