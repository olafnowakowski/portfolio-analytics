# Key Takeaways: Gathering and Analyzing Business Requirements

## Main Topic

This section explains why gathering and analyzing business requirements is important, what requirements elicitation means, and the main categories and techniques used to collect requirements.

---

## Why Gathering and Analyzing Requirements Is Important

**Business requirements** are specific conditions provided by stakeholders that a solution must meet in order to solve a business problem.

Gathering and analyzing requirements is important because it helps make sure the final product, service, or process:

- solves the business problem
- meets stakeholder needs
- supports company objectives
- reduces misunderstandings
- prevents costly mistakes
- avoids delays and project failure

### Important Lesson

If requirements are not clear, the team may build the wrong solution or misunderstand what stakeholders actually need.

---

## The Four Categories of Business Requirements

Business requirements can be grouped into four main categories:

1. **Functional Requirements**
2. **Operational Requirements**
3. **Technical Requirements**
4. **Transitional Requirements**

---

## 1. Functional Requirements

### Meaning

Functional requirements explain how a product, service, or solution should work from the end user’s point of view.

They describe the features and functions that the user directly interacts with.

### Example

The website should be user-friendly and allow smooth navigation.

### Simple Explanation

Functional requirements describe what the user can do with the product or system.

---

## 2. Operational Requirements

### Meaning

Operational requirements describe the background procedures needed for the product or process to keep working over time.

### Example

The website should be able to handle a high volume of traffic and transactions.

### Simple Explanation

Operational requirements focus on making sure the system runs properly and reliably.

---

## 3. Technical Requirements

### Meaning

Technical requirements describe the technical challenges or conditions that must be addressed to successfully build or implement the product or process.

### Example

The website should offer multiple payment options to customers.

### Simple Explanation

Technical requirements focus on the technology, systems, or technical capabilities needed for the solution.

---

## 4. Transitional Requirements

### Meaning

Transitional requirements describe the steps needed to move from the old process or system to the new one.

### Example

The website must be fully tested before launch.

### Simple Explanation

Transitional requirements help make sure the new solution can be introduced successfully.

---

## Requirements Elicitation

### What It Means

**Requirements elicitation** is the process of gathering business needs from stakeholders.

During elicitation, a business analyst may:

- interview stakeholders
- analyze business processes
- review documents
- gather feedback
- clarify business needs
- define what a solution should accomplish

---

## The Requirements Elicitation Process

The requirements elicitation process has four main steps:

1. **Preparation**
2. **Elicitation**
3. **Analysis**
4. **Validation**

---

## 1. Preparation

### What Happens

The analyst prepares by learning about the company and the context of the problem.

This includes:

- learning the inner workings of the company
- understanding company KPIs
- reviewing business rules and documents
- analyzing stakeholders using the power/interest grid

### Purpose

Preparation helps the analyst understand the business before asking stakeholders for requirements.

---

## 2. Elicitation

### What Happens

The analyst interacts directly with stakeholders to gather requirements.

This may include:

- interviews
- focus groups
- surveys
- brainstorming
- other elicitation techniques

### Purpose

The goal is to understand stakeholder needs, pain points, and expectations.

---

## 3. Analysis

### What Happens

The analyst reviews the requirements gathered during elicitation.

This includes:

- identifying useful requirements
- removing requirements outside the project scope
- resolving vague requirements
- reconciling conflicting requirements
- settling on a specific set of requirements

### Purpose

Analysis turns a large amount of feedback into a clear and useful list of requirements.

---

## 4. Validation

### What Happens

The analyst confirms that each requirement should be included in the final list.

For each requirement, the analyst should answer:

- Why is this requirement needed?
- What exactly does it mean?
- How could it be implemented?
- When should it be implemented?

### Purpose

Validation makes sure the final requirements are clear, realistic, and connected to the business problem.

---

## Examples of Elicitation Techniques

### 1. Interviewing

Interviews allow analysts to have in-depth conversations with stakeholders.

This is one of the most common elicitation techniques.

Interviews help analysts:

- ask detailed questions
- understand stakeholder needs
- learn about pain points
- clarify confusing information
- gather deeper insights

---

### 2. Brainstorming

Brainstorming brings stakeholders together to generate ideas.

It helps analysts:

- collect many ideas
- identify common themes
- discover creative possibilities
- understand different perspectives

The analyst must keep the session focused so it does not become disorganized.

---

### 3. Interface Analysis

Interface analysis allows analysts to observe how users interact with a product, process, or system.

It helps identify:

- user behavior
- pain points
- usability problems
- poor functionality
- areas that need improvement

---

### Other Possible Techniques

Other useful elicitation techniques include:

- focus groups
- surveys
- questionnaires
- prototyping

## Big Lesson

Gathering and analyzing requirements is one of the most important parts of business analysis because it helps the team understand what the solution must do before building or implementing anything.

Clear requirements help prevent confusion, reduce project risk, and make sure the final solution actually meets stakeholder and business needs.

---

## Final Summary

Business requirements are the specific conditions a solution must meet to solve a business problem. Gathering and analyzing these requirements helps ensure that the final product or process supports stakeholder needs and company objectives. Requirements elicitation is the process of gathering those needs through methods such as interviews, brainstorming, surveys, focus groups, prototyping, and interface analysis. The process includes preparation, elicitation, analysis, and validation. Requirements can be categorized as functional, operational, technical, or transitional. Clear and validated requirements help teams build better solutions and avoid costly mistakes.
