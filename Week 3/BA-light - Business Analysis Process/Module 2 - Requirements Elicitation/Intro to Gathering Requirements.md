# Business Requirement Preparation

## Main Idea

In the previous module, the focus was on the first step of the business analysis process framework:

- **Problem Assessment**

Now the focus moves to the second step:

- **Business Requirement Preparation**

This step is very important because it should happen **before** deep analysis or solution implementation begins.

Business requirement preparation helps business analysts understand exactly what a solution must do in order to solve the business problem.

---

## Business Analysis Process Framework Reminder

The business analysis process framework includes three main parts:

1. **Problem Assessment**
2. **Business Requirement Preparation**
3. **Solution Implementation**

In this module, the focus is on **Business Requirement Preparation**.

This part includes:

- gather and analyze business requirements
- create supporting visual models
- document business requirements

---

# What Are Business Requirements?

## Definition

**Business requirements** are specific conditions provided by stakeholders that a solution must meet in order to solve a business problem.

In simple words, business requirements explain what the solution needs to do to be successful.

---

## Why Business Requirements Matter

Gathering and analyzing business requirements is critical because it helps make sure the final product, service, or process:

- solves the business problem
- meets stakeholder needs
- aligns with company objectives
- focuses on the most important priorities
- avoids misunderstandings
- avoids delays and expensive mistakes

Without clear requirements, teams may build the wrong thing or solve the wrong problem.

---

# Why Clear Requirements Add Value

Clear business requirements help the project team:

- understand what the business needs
- prioritize important work
- focus on stakeholder and customer needs
- avoid miscommunication
- reduce the risk of project failure
- create a roadmap for action

### Important Lesson

A business cannot effectively achieve its objectives if it has not clearly defined what it needs.

---

# Gathering Business Requirements

## What It Means

Gathering requirements means collecting information from stakeholders about what the solution should do.

The methods used depend on:

- the nature of the project
- the project scope
- stakeholder preferences
- available time and resources

---

## Common Requirement-Gathering Techniques

### 1. Interviews

Interviews allow business analysts to ask stakeholders questions directly.

They help analysts understand:

- business processes
- pain points
- stakeholder needs
- expectations for the final product or service

### Why interviews are useful

They provide detailed insights from people who are directly involved in the process.

---

### 2. Surveys

Surveys allow analysts to gather information from many stakeholders quickly.

Surveys can be sent through:

- email
- online forms
- other digital tools

### Why surveys are useful

They are helpful when the analyst needs feedback from a large group of people in a short amount of time.

---

### 3. Focus Groups

A focus group is a small group of stakeholders who provide feedback and insights about the project.

Focus groups can help:

- validate requirements
- test possible solutions
- understand different stakeholder opinions

---

# Using Problem Assessment to Guide Requirement Questions

The first step of the business analysis process, **Problem Assessment**, helps determine what questions should be asked during requirement gathering.

Problem assessment gives the analyst enough background to ask better discovery questions.

---

## Example: Customer Service Problem

Imagine a company has problems with:

- long customer service response times
- low customer satisfaction

A business analyst could ask questions such as:

- What channels do customers use to contact customer service?
  - phone
  - email
  - chat
- Are there common customer complaints or issues that happen often?
- How are customer service representatives trained to handle questions and complaints?

---

## Gathering Multiple Perspectives

For this customer service example, feedback could be gathered from:

- customer service associates through interviews
- dissatisfied customers through surveys

### Why this matters

This helps the analyst understand the problem from both sides:

- the business side
- the customer side

---

# Analyzing Business Requirements

## What It Means

After gathering stakeholder feedback, the analyst must analyze the requirements.

This means deciding:

- which requirements are most important
- which requirements are realistic
- which requirements fit within the project constraints
- which requirements should be prioritized first

---

## Why Prioritization Matters

Prioritization makes sure that time, money, and effort are focused on the most important needs first.

This helps deliver a solution that:

- addresses the biggest concerns
- provides the most value
- aligns with stakeholder priorities

---

## Why Feasibility Matters

Feasibility means checking whether a requirement can realistically be completed within the project’s limits.

Common constraints include:

- budget
- timeline
- available resources
- technical ability
- staffing

### Important Lesson

A requirement may be useful, but if it cannot be completed within the project constraints, it may need to be delayed, reduced, or removed from scope.

---

# Example: Revamping an E-Commerce Website

Imagine a company wants to redesign or improve its e-commerce website.

After interviewing stakeholders, the analyst gathers these requirements:

- The website should be user-friendly and allow smooth navigation.
- The website should handle a high volume of traffic and transactions.
- The website should offer multiple payment options.
- The website must be fully tested before launch.

---

## Prioritizing the Requirements

The analyst needs to decide which requirements matter most and which are realistic.

### Highest priority: User-friendly website

This may be the top priority because it affects:

- every user
- the customer experience
- sales
- conversion rates

### Important but maybe lower priority: High traffic volume

Handling high traffic is important, but depending on project constraints, the company may choose to support a smaller traffic threshold at first.

### Extra feature: Multiple payment options

Multiple payment options may be useful, but they may not be necessary for the first version of the project.

This requirement might fall outside the project scope.

### Required before launch: Full testing

Testing is important because the website must work correctly before customers use it.

---

# Why Requirement Analysis Helps

By analyzing requirements, the business analyst can create a clear roadmap for other teams.

This roadmap helps teams:

- understand what needs to be built
- know what to prioritize
- avoid unnecessary work
- reduce confusion
- prevent expensive fixes after launch

### Important Lesson

It is much cheaper and easier to define requirements clearly before building the product than to fix problems after the product has already launched.

# Requirement Categories

Requirements can be organized into four main categories:

1. **Functional Requirements**
2. **Operational Requirements**
3. **Technical Requirements**
4. **Transitional Requirements**

Organizing requirements into categories makes analysis easier.

---

## 1. Functional Requirements

### Definition

Functional requirements describe what a product, service, or solution should do from the end user’s point of view.

They explain the features and functions that users will directly interact with.

### Example

The website should be user-friendly and allow a smooth navigation experience.

### Simple meaning

Functional requirements describe the visible features users need.

---

## 2. Operational Requirements

### Definition

Operational requirements describe procedures that must happen in the background so the product or process can continue working over time.

### Example

The website should be able to handle a high volume of traffic and transactions.

### Simple meaning

Operational requirements describe what is needed to keep the system working reliably.

---

## 3. Technical Requirements

### Definition

Technical requirements describe technical challenges or conditions that must be addressed in order to successfully implement the process or product.

### Example

The website should offer multiple payment options to customers.

### Simple meaning

Technical requirements describe the technology or system capabilities needed.

---

## 4. Transitional Requirements

### Definition

Transitional requirements describe procedures that must happen in order to put a new product, service, or process into place.

### Example

The website must be fully tested before launch.

### Simple meaning

Transitional requirements help move from the old process or system to the new one.

---

# Requirement Categories Table

| Category | Definition | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Functional Requirements** | Conditions that specify how a product, service, or solution should work from the end user’s point of view. They explain the features and functions the end user directly engages with. | The website should be user-friendly and allow a smooth navigation experience. |
| **Operational Requirements** | Procedures that must be performed in the background so the product or process can function over time. | The website should be able to handle a high volume of traffic and transactions. |
| **Technical Requirements** | Challenges that must be addressed in order to successfully implement the process or manufacture the product. | The website should offer multiple payment options to customers. |
| **Transitional Requirements** | Procedures that must be executed in order to put the new product or process into place. | The website must be fully tested prior to launch. |

# Final Business Requirements Report

After gathering and analyzing requirements, the analyst should provide:

- primary findings
- a full report on the company’s requirements

This should usually be delivered as a **written document**.

The written report helps make the requirements clear and accessible for stakeholders and project teams.

---

# Big Lesson

Business requirement preparation helps turn a business problem into clear, actionable needs.

It is the bridge between understanding the problem and building a solution.

A strong requirements process helps ensure the final solution actually solves the problem and meets stakeholder expectations.

---

# Key Takeaways

- Business requirement preparation is the second step in the business analysis process framework.
- This step should happen before deep analysis or solution implementation.
- Business requirements are specific conditions a solution must meet to solve a business problem.
- Gathering requirements helps understand stakeholder needs and company objectives.
- Clear requirements reduce misunderstandings, delays, costly mistakes, and project failures.
- Common ways to gather requirements include interviews, surveys, and focus groups.
- Problem assessment helps guide the discovery questions used in requirement gathering.
- Requirements should be analyzed for priority and feasibility.
- Prioritization helps focus resources on the most important requirements.
- Feasibility checks whether requirements can be completed within budget, timeline, and resource limits.
- Requirements can be grouped into functional, operational, technical, and transitional categories.
- After analysis, the business analyst should document findings and requirements in a written report.

---

# Extra Important Notes

- Stakeholders are the main source of many business requirements.
- Requirements should be clear before building or implementing a solution.
- Some requirements may be useful but still fall outside the project scope.
- Requirement categories help organize complex information.
- A strong requirements document can prevent teams from building a solution that does not meet business needs.
- Business analysts help translate stakeholder feedback into clear instructions for action.

# Final Summary

Business requirement preparation is a critical part of the business analysis process. It involves gathering and analyzing the specific conditions a solution must meet in order to solve a business problem. Analysts gather requirements through interviews, surveys, focus groups, and other methods, then analyze them based on priority and feasibility. Requirements can be grouped into functional, operational, technical, and transitional categories. Clear requirements help prevent miscommunication, reduce project risk, and create a roadmap for teams to build solutions that meet stakeholder needs and company objectives.
