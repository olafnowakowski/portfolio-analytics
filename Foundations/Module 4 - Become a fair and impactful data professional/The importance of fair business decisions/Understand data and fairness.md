**Fairness in Data Analysis**
- Fairness means ensuring that data analysis does not create or reinforce bias, aiming to create systems that are fair and inclusive.
> There is no single standard definition of fairness, but it involves considering social context and avoiding unfair conclusions.

**Challenges of Fairness**
- Data conclusions can be true but still unfair if they ignore important social factors or context.
- Example: A company concludes only men succeed based on performance data, but this ignores toxic culture and barriers faced by other genders.

**Ethical Data Analysis Practices**
- Fair conclusions consider underlying social issues and systemic factors affecting data outcomes.
- Analysts should factor in social context from data collection to presenting conclusions to stakeholders.

**Example of Fair Data Analysis**
- Harvard data scientists working on cardiovascular risk in the Stroke Belt prioritized fairness by collaborating with social scientists.
- They used methods like oversampling underrepresented groups and collecting self-reported data to reduce bias.
