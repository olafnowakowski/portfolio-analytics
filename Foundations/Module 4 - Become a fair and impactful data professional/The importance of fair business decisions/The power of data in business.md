# Common theme ​across every example
**Issue**
- A topic or subject to investigate

**Question**
- Designed to discover information

**Problem**
- An obstacle or complication that needs to be worked out/solved

# Examples and Application of Business Tasks
- Coca-Cola used data analysis to answer a question about customer preferences for new product flavors.
- The City Zoo and Aquarium faced a staffing problem that data analysis helped solve by identifying optimal staffing strategies.
> **Business task**
> - The question or problem data analysis answers for a business

# The city Zoo example of business task
- Analyze weather data from ​the last decade to identify predictable patterns. ​
- The data analysts could then ​plan out the best way to gather, ​analyze, and present the data needed ​to solve this task and meet the zoos goals. ​Then, using data, the zoo would be able to make ​informed decisions about their daily staffing.

# Data-Driven Decision Making and Its Importance
- Data-driven decision making uses facts discovered through data analysis to guide business strategies.
- The zoo example illustrates how analyzing weather data helped solve staffing challenges by making informed decisions.
> Relying solely on observation and intuition provides an incomplete picture, whereas data offers a comprehensive view that enables better solutions.
