**Fairness means ensuring your analysis doesn't create or reinforce bias**

**Consider all of the available data**
- A data analyst should look at all useful data, not just the data that supports what they expect.
- Sometimes there is data that seems unimportant or does not match the main pattern.
- Even if it feels unrelated at first, it may still matter for the analysis.
- Ignoring data can lead to biased or incomplete conclusions.
- The goal is to make sure the results show the truth, not just personal assumptions.
> Main idea:
> - Use all relevant data so the analysis is accurate and not based only on expectations.

**Identify surrounding factors**
- Data does not exist by itself; it is affected by context.
- Surrounding factors are outside conditions or influences that can affect the results.
- These factors help stakeholders better understand why the results happened.
- Looking only at the raw numbers may miss important reasons behind the conclusion.
- Considering context makes the analysis more complete and fair.
> Main idea:
> - Understand the situation around the data, because context can change how conclusions should be interpreted.

**Include self-reported data**
- Self-reporting means people give information about themselves and their own experiences.
- This is useful because it reduces the chance that someone else’s opinion or judgment changes the data.
- It helps lower conscious bias and unconscious bias from observers.
- It also gives direct insight from the people being studied.
- Self-reported data can add important context to other collected data.
> Main idea:
> - Let people describe their own experiences to reduce observer bias and improve fairness.

**Use oversampling effectively**
- Sometimes some groups are underrepresented in a dataset.
- If a group does not have enough data, the analysis may not reflect them fairly.
- Oversampling means increasing the sample size of those underrepresented groups.
- This helps create a dataset that better represents the full population.
- It is used to address imbalanced datasets and improve fairness.
> Main idea:
> - Collect more data from underrepresented groups so they are represented fairly in the analysis.

**Think about fairness from beginning to end**
- Fairness should not be checked only at the end of a project.
- It should be considered during every step of the process.
- This includes:
  - data collection
  - data cleaning
  - data processing
  - data analysis
  - final use of insights
- A project is more fair when fairness is built in from the start until the organization acts on the data insights.
> Main idea:
> - Fairness is part of the whole project, not just one step.
