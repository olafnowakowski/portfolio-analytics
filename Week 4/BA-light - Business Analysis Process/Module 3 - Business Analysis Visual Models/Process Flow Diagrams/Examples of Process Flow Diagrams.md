# Types of Process Diagrams

## Main Idea

A **process diagram** is a visual model that shows how a business process works.

So far, the main type discussed has been the **process flowchart**, but Business Analysts may use different kinds of process diagrams depending on the situation.

The goal is always the same:

**Help stakeholders understand a process more clearly.**

---

# 1. Process Flowchart

## What It Is

A **process flowchart** is a visual representation of the sequence of steps and decisions needed to complete a process.

It is the most common type of business process map.
<img width="300" height="300" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/53c35023-b55c-44ec-9c5a-241c0a1465f4" />

## What It Shows

A process flowchart can show:

- the start of a process
- each process step
- decision points
- different possible paths
- the end of the process

## Why It Is Useful

Process flowcharts are useful because they make a process easy to follow from beginning to end.

### Simple Explanation

A process flowchart answers:

**What happens first, what happens next, and how does the process end?**

---

# 2. Swimlane Diagram

## What It Is

A **swimlane diagram** is similar to a flowchart, but it separates the process steps by the person, team, or department responsible for each step.

It is also called a **cross-functional map**.
<img width="1732" height="898" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/339142fd-5b26-482b-b709-600c3a22359e" />

## What Makes It Different

The main difference is that a swimlane diagram shows responsibility.

Each “lane” belongs to a different:

- person
- team
- department
- role
- system

## Why It Is Useful

A swimlane diagram clearly shows:

- who does each task
- where handoffs happen
- which teams are involved
- where responsibility changes
- where delays or confusion may happen

### Important Lesson

Swimlane diagrams are especially useful when a process involves multiple teams or departments.

---

# 3. Value Stream Map

## What It Is

A **value stream map** is a process diagram often used in **lean management**.

Lean management focuses on continuous improvement by making small, systematic changes to improve efficiency.
<img width="1744" height="956" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/62d9c816-7d87-4ea1-826e-7a4de24d3bd1" />

## What It Shows

A value stream map analyzes all the steps needed to:

- create a product
- deliver a service
- move from request to final delivery

It often includes more detail than a regular flowchart.

## Example Categories

A value stream map may organize steps into categories such as:

- information flow
- product flow
- time ladder

## Why It Is Useful

Value stream maps help show the big picture of a process.

They are useful for finding:

- waste
- delays
- inefficiencies
- repeated steps
- process bottlenecks

### Important Note

Value stream maps are usually more complex than flowcharts and may not be as easy to understand at a quick glance.

---

# 4. SIPOC Diagram

## What It Is

A **SIPOC diagram** is a high-level process map that focuses on the essentials of a process.

SIPOC stands for:

- **Suppliers**
- **Inputs**
- **Process**
- **Outputs**
- **Customers**
<img width="1672" height="962" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/a935a6c9-6c51-42aa-bd56-00945a148516" />

## What It Shows

A SIPOC diagram shows:

- who provides resources
- what inputs are needed
- what process happens
- what outputs are created
- who receives the outputs

## Why It Is Useful

SIPOC diagrams are useful when you need a simple, high-level overview of a process.

### Simple Explanation

A SIPOC diagram answers:

**Who gives us what, what do we do with it, what comes out, and who receives it?**

---

# Comparison of Process Diagram Types

| Diagram Type | Best Used For | Main Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| **Process Flowchart** | Showing the sequence of steps and decisions in a process | Easy to follow from start to end |
| **Swimlane Diagram** | Showing which person, team, or department owns each step | Clarifies responsibilities |
| **Value Stream Map** | Analyzing complex processes for efficiency and waste | Shows the big picture and improvement opportunities |
| **SIPOC Diagram** | Giving a high-level overview of a process | Focuses on suppliers, inputs, process, outputs, and customers |

---

# Many Other Process Diagrams Exist

There are many other types of process diagrams used in business analysis.

Different companies and teams may use different templates depending on:

- the process being analyzed
- the level of detail needed
- the audience
- the project goal
- company standards

A simple online search can show many different ways to visually organize business processes.

---

# Choosing the Right Diagram

The most important thing is to choose the visual model that fits your purpose.

Ask:

- Do I need to show the process sequence?
- Do I need to show who is responsible?
- Do I need to analyze efficiency and waste?
- Do I need a high-level overview?
- Who will read this diagram?
- How much detail do stakeholders need?

### Important Lesson

The best process diagram is the one that helps stakeholders understand the process clearly.

---

# Big Lesson

There is no single perfect process diagram for every situation.

Business Analysts choose the type of diagram based on what they need to explain or analyze.

---

# Key Takeaways

- A process flowchart shows the sequence of steps and decisions in a process.
- A process flowchart is the most common type of business process map.
- A swimlane diagram separates steps by the team, person, or department responsible.
- Swimlane diagrams are useful for showing roles and responsibilities.
- A value stream map is often used in lean management to analyze efficiency and waste.
- Value stream maps are usually more complex than basic flowcharts.
- A SIPOC diagram gives a simple high-level view of suppliers, inputs, processes, outputs, and customers.
- Many other process diagram types exist.
- The purpose of a visual model is to help stakeholders understand processes more clearly.
- Business Analysts should choose the diagram type that best fits the goal of the analysis.

---

# Extra Important Notes

- Process diagrams are communication tools, not just drawings.
- A simple diagram may be better than a complex one if the audience needs a quick overview.
- A detailed diagram may be better when analyzing inefficiencies or responsibilities.
- The diagram should match the business problem being studied.
- Visual models should make the process easier to understand, not harder.

This lesson explains several types of process diagrams used in business analysis. The most common is the process flowchart, which shows the sequence of steps and decisions in a process.
