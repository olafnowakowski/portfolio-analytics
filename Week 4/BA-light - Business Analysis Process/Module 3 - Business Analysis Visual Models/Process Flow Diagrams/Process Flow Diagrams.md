# Purpose of Process Flow Diagrams

## Main Idea

A **process flow diagram**, also called a **process map**, helps a Business Analyst understand how a process is executed inside an organization.

The goal is to clearly see how the process works so it can be improved.

---

# What Is a Process Flow Diagram?

A **process flow diagram** is a visual model that shows the steps of a business process from beginning to end.

It helps show:

- what happens in the process
- where the process starts
- where the process ends
- what steps happen in between
- who or what is involved
- where decisions are made
- where problems or delays may exist

---

# Why Process Flow Diagrams Matter

Process flow diagrams are important because they help teams:

- understand specific parts of the business
- brainstorm ideas for improvement
- improve communication
- create standard documentation
- see inefficiencies in a process
- identify unclear responsibilities
- understand process ownership
- find delays or repeated steps

---

# Main Purpose

The main purpose of a process flow diagram is to help you understand how something is done in the organization so you can improve its efficiency.

In simple words:

**You cannot improve a process if you cannot clearly see how it works.**

---

# Goals of a Process Flow Diagram

As a Business Analyst, you create process flow diagrams to achieve three main goals:

1. **Show a process from beginning to end**
2. **Break processes into easy-to-follow steps and symbols**
3. **Analyze how a process can be improved**

---

# 1. Show a Process From Beginning to End

## Meaning

A process flow diagram should clearly show the full process, from the first step to the final outcome.

This helps everyone understand the complete journey of the process.

## Why It Matters

Clearly defining the boundaries of the process early helps avoid confusion.

You need to know:

- where the process starts
- where the process ends
- what is included
- what is not included

## Example

If you are analyzing shipping workflows to find inefficiencies, focus only on the specific process of how a product gets from the factory floor to the customer’s door.

Do not include unrelated processes that are not directly connected.

### Important Lesson

Define the process boundaries early so the map stays focused.

---

# 2. Break Processes Into Easy-to-Follow Steps and Symbols

## Meaning

A process map should be clear not only to the person creating it, but also to anyone who looks at it.

The process should be broken into small, understandable steps.

## Why It Matters

A confusing process map is not useful.

A good process map uses simple symbols and clear steps so people across the organization can understand it.

## Common Symbols

Process maps often use standard symbols such as:

- **Oval**: start or end of the process
- **Rectangle**: process step or action
- **Diamond**: decision point
- **Arrow**: direction or flow of the process

### Important Lesson

The goal is to make the process easy to follow for everyone, not just the analyst.

---

# 3. Analyze How a Process Can Be Improved

## Meaning

One of the main reasons for creating a process map is to identify ways the process can be improved.

A process map helps show the important characteristics of the process and where weaknesses may exist.

## What You Can Look For

A process map can help identify:

- repeated steps
- delays
- bottlenecks
- unclear responsibilities
- unnecessary approvals
- inefficient handoffs
- process gaps
- confusing decision points

### Important Lesson

You cannot fix what you cannot see.

Visualizing the process makes it easier to find improvement opportunities.

---

# Inefficiencies a Process Map Can Reveal

A process map can help identify areas where the process is inefficient.

Examples include:

- repeated work
- unnecessary steps
- delays between departments
- unclear ownership
- unclear responsibilities
- poor communication between teams
- missing decision points
- bottlenecks

---

# Process Boundaries

## What Are Process Boundaries?

Process boundaries define what is included and excluded in the process map.

They help keep the analysis focused.

## Why Boundaries Matter

Without clear boundaries, the process map can become too broad and confusing.

For example, if you are studying shipping inefficiencies, you should not map unrelated processes like product design or marketing unless they directly affect shipping.

---

# Process Ownership and Responsibilities

Process maps can also reveal problems with ownership and responsibility.

They can help answer:

- Who owns the process?
- Who performs each step?
- Who makes decisions?
- Who approves work?
- Who is responsible when something goes wrong?

### Important Lesson

If ownership or responsibility is unclear, the process may become inefficient.

---

# Standard Documentation

Process maps also provide standard documentation that everyone can use.

This helps:

- new employees learn the process
- teams stay aligned
- stakeholders understand the workflow
- managers identify improvement areas
- analysts communicate findings clearly

---

# Big Lesson

Process flow diagrams help Business Analysts understand how a process currently works so they can identify where it can be improved.

They make complicated processes easier to see, explain, and analyze.

---

# Key Takeaways

- Process flow diagrams are also called process maps.
- They help show how a process is executed in an organization.
- The goal is to understand the process so it can be improved.
- A process map should show the process from beginning to end.
- The boundaries of the process should be defined early.
- Processes should be broken into clear steps and standard symbols.
- Good process maps are understandable to everyone in the organization.
- Process maps help identify inefficiencies, delays, repetitions, and bottlenecks.
- They can also reveal unclear ownership or responsibilities.
- Process maps improve communication and provide standard documentation.
- A process cannot be improved effectively unless it is clearly understood.

---

# Final Summary

A process flow diagram is a visual tool that helps Business Analysts understand how a business process works from start to finish. It shows the steps, decisions, and flow of work in a clear and simple way. Process maps help define process boundaries, break complex work into easy-to-follow steps, and identify areas for improvement. They are useful for finding inefficiencies, repeated steps, delays, bottlenecks, and unclear responsibilities. The main lesson is that process flow diagrams help teams see the current process clearly so they can improve it effectively.
