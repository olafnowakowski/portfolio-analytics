### True or false? A business domain model is a visual representation of business processes and how each piece relates to one another.
- True
> A business domain model is often used to clarify high-level connections between different business processes.

### Explain how a “business domain model” is different from a “process flow diagram.”
- A process flow diagram illustrates a particular business process, showing step-by-step how that process is carried out. A business domain model, on the other hand, shows how different elements of a business relate to one another without necessarily getting into the specifics of any particular process.

### In a business domain model, what is the term used to describe the different parts of the business that will be identified in the visual (such as customers, products, and orders)?
- Classes
> In a business domain model, the different parts of the business that you are identifying and visualizing are called classes. In the diagram, classes are housed in boxes.

### In a business domain model, what is the term used to describe how each part of the business connects to another?
- Associations
> In a business domain model, associations show how each part of the business connects to another. In the diagram, associations are shown by a line connecting boxes.

### Let’s say that you have a business domain model. At the top of one of the boxes in the diagram, you have the word “CUSTOMER.” Below that, you have a list: “customerID,” “firstName,” “lastName.” What is the term used to refer to this list of information or data? 
- Attributes
> In a business domain model, attributes are added to each box to show what information or data is available for each part of the business included in the diagram.
