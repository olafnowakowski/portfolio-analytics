# Visual Models in Business Analysis

## Main Idea

After gathering and analyzing business requirements, the next important step is creating **supporting visual models**.

Visual models help Business Analysts understand and explain complex business processes more clearly than long blocks of text.

They make it easier to see:

- how a process works
- who is involved
- where decisions happen
- where problems or gaps exist
- how requirements connect to the business process

---

## What Are Visual Models?

**Visual models** are charts, images, diagrams, or flow diagrams that organize and display business requirements visually.

They are one of the most useful tools in a Business Analyst’s toolbox because they help turn complex information into something easier to understand.

Examples of visual models include:

- flowcharts
- swimlane diagrams
- domain models
- process maps
- workflow diagrams

---

# Flowcharts

## What Is a Flowchart?

A **flowchart** is a diagram that shows the steps in a process.

Each block represents a step, and arrows show the direction of movement through the process.

In the example from the reading, a hospital may use a flowchart to show its food delivery process.

# Why Visual Models Are Important

Visual models are useful for several reasons:

1. They are easier to understand than long text.
2. They help discover missing requirements.
3. They reduce misunderstandings within a team.
4. They help stakeholders engage with the process.
5. They make complex systems easier to explain.

---

# 1. A Picture Is Worth a Thousand Words

## Main Point

Visual models can replace long and confusing text explanations.

Instead of writing pages of instructions, a Business Analyst can use a diagram to show the same information more clearly.

---

## Example: Map App

A map app is a common example of a useful visual model.

---

## Why This Matters in Business Analysis

Business processes can be complex.

If requirements are explained only through long text, stakeholders may:

- lose focus
- misunderstand details
- miss important steps
- struggle to see how everything connects

A visual model helps stakeholders understand the process faster.

---

# Keep It Visual and Simple

Visual models should be clear and simple.

They should help people understand the process, not make it more confusing.

Good visual models include enough detail to be useful but avoid unnecessary complexity.

### Important Lesson

A strong visual model explains the process clearly without overwhelming the audience.

---

# Swimlane Diagrams

## What Is a Swimlane Diagram?

A **swimlane diagram** is a type of visual model that shows which person, team, or department is responsible for each part of a process.

The process is divided into lanes, and each lane represents a role or business area.

---

## What Swimlane Diagrams Show

A swimlane diagram can show:

- process steps
- decision points
- responsibilities
- handoffs between teams
- where different departments are involved

In the example from the reading, a swimlane diagram shows the business lines involved in getting a product successfully to a customer.

---

## Decision Points

Swimlane diagrams often include decision points.

For example:

**Order Complete?**

Depending on the answer, the process may continue in different directions.

### Important Lesson

Swimlane diagrams are useful because they show both the process flow and who is responsible for each step.

---

# 2. Discover Gaps in Requirements

## Main Point

Missing requirements are one of the most common causes of project failure.

Visual models can help reveal missing pieces that may be hard to notice in written text.

---

## Why Text Can Hide Gaps

When requirements are written as text blocks or lists, it can be hard to see how everything connects.

A Business Analyst may miss:

- missing steps
- unclear handoffs
- missing stakeholders
- incomplete requirements
- broken connections between process parts

## Map Example Again

# Business Analysis as a Journey

A business analysis process can be compared to map directions.

The goal is to reach a specific business outcome.

A visual model helps show:

- where the process starts
- what steps are required
- where decisions happen
- where the process ends
- whether anything is missing

---

# Domain Models

## What Is a Domain Model?

A **domain model** gives a high-level, “bird’s-eye view” of company-wide processes or relationships.

Domain models can become large and complex, but they are useful for understanding how different parts of the business connect.

---

## Why Domain Models Are Useful

Domain models help Business Analysts:

- understand the larger business environment
- see relationships between processes
- find gaps in business requirements
- identify areas that need improvement
- understand how different parts of the business interact

### Important Lesson

Domain models are useful when you need to understand the big picture of a business process or system.

---

# 3. Minimize Group Misconceptions

## Main Point

Visual models help reduce misunderstandings among project team members and stakeholders.

Even one misunderstanding can cause:

- delays
- mistakes
- confusion
- rework
- project problems

---

## Why Misunderstandings Happen

Long text documents can be difficult to understand.

Some team members may not fully understand the requirements but may avoid speaking up.

This can happen because they:

- feel embarrassed
- do not want to slow the group down
- think others understand better
- are less vocal in meetings
- are unsure how to ask questions

As a result, the Business Analyst may wrongly think everyone understands the requirements.

---

## How Visual Models Help

Visual models are easier to engage with than long text documents.

Stakeholders can look at a diagram and ask questions such as:

- What happens at this step?
- Who is responsible here?
- Why does the process split here?
- What happens if the answer is no?
- Is this step missing something?

### Important Lesson

Visual models make it easier for people to ask questions and clarify misunderstandings.

---

# Visual Models Support Different Knowledge Levels

Project teams often include people with different levels of understanding.

Some may know the process deeply, while others may only understand part of it.

Visual models help everyone engage at their own level.

They allow both technical and non-technical stakeholders to participate in the conversation.

---

# Benefits of Visual Models

Visual models help Business Analysts:

- explain complex information clearly
- reduce long text explanations
- make requirements easier to understand
- identify missing requirements
- reveal process gaps
- show decision points
- clarify responsibilities
- reduce misunderstandings
- support stakeholder discussions
- improve team alignment
