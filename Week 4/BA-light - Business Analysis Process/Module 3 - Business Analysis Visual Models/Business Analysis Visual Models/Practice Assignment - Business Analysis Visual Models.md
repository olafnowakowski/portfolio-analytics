### Which of the following is NOT considered a benefit of using visuals in business analysis documentation? 
- Visual models can replace written explanations of the analysis.
> Visual models are not meant to replace written analysis. Instead, they serve as a tool to support the analysis and make information easier to understand.

### True or false? Creating a visual model can help an analyst to discover gaps in the requirements that have been gathered from stakeholders.
- True
> Especially in a large company, various processes can become large and complex, with lots of moving parts between different departments. By mapping out a visual model of the process in question, an analyst may be able to spot areas that the stakeholders didn’t include in their original list of requirements. 

---

### What are some ways in which using a visual model can help to minimize group misconceptions? 
- Especially with long blocks of text, people who feel they do not fully understand what they are reading may be less likely to speak up or ask questions. Less vocal members of the team may pull back and fail to voice concerns, leading the analyst to mistakenly believe that everyone understands and agrees with the project requirements.
Visual models provide a more accessible option for people to engage with; it’s much easier to ask questions about a visually arranged model than a text document.

### True or false? Once you’ve collected requirements from stakeholders, the next step is to create a process map that shows your suggested new-and-improved process.
- False
> Before you can understand where your company needs to improve, you should map the business processes as they currently are. Process maps put you in a better position to understand how specific aspects of the business function so you can analyze their strengths and weaknesses.
