# Business Process Mapping

## Main Idea

To improve a business process, you first need to understand how the process currently works.

A Business Analyst should not jump straight into solutions. First, they should map the current process so they can clearly see:

- what happens
- who is involved
- where decisions are made
- where problems might exist
- where improvement opportunities may be found

---

# Why Do We Need to Map Business Processes?

## Key Idea

To know where you are going, you must first understand where you are.

Before improving a process, the analyst must understand the **current state** of that process.

This means understanding:

- the steps involved
- the order of those steps
- the people or teams involved
- the decisions made during the process
- the problems or inefficiencies in the process

---

# Process Flow Diagram

## Definition

A **process flow diagram** is a flowchart that shows a specific business process being analyzed.

It visually represents the steps in a process and shows how work moves from one step to the next.

---

## What a Process Flow Diagram Shows

A process flow diagram can show:

- process steps
- decision points
- actions taken after each decision
- the order of activities
- how the process starts and ends
- where the process may branch into different paths

---

## Why It Helps

A process flow diagram helps the Business Analyst understand the inner workings of a process.

It is useful because it makes it easier to see how the process functions instead of trying to understand everything from text alone.

### Important Lesson

A visual process map is usually much clearer than a long written explanation.

---

# Process Flow vs Process Map

The terms **process flow** and **process map** are often used interchangeably.

However, there is a small difference:

## Process Mapping

**Process mapping** is the act of creating the visual model.

## Process Flow Diagram

A **process flow diagram** or **process flowchart** is the final visual model created from the mapping process.

### Simple Explanation

- Process mapping = the activity
- Process flow diagram = the finished diagram

# Why Process Mapping Matters

Process mapping is important in any business or organization because it helps analysts understand how work is currently being done.

Before identifying improvements, the analyst should map the process as it exists now.

This is sometimes called understanding the **current state**.

---

# Current State Process

## Meaning

The **current state process** is how the process works right now.

It may include:

- formal steps
- informal steps
- delays
- inefficiencies
- repeated work
- unclear handoffs
- unnecessary approvals

### Important Lesson

You cannot improve a process effectively if you do not understand how it currently works.

---

# Benefits of Process Mapping

Process maps help Business Analysts:

- understand business processes
- identify strengths and weaknesses
- see where work slows down
- find unnecessary steps
- understand decision points
- clarify responsibilities
- communicate process details to stakeholders
- support better analysis and improvement

---

# Stakeholder Input

Before creating a process map, the Business Analyst should talk to important stakeholders.

Stakeholders can explain:

- how the process actually works
- where problems occur
- who is responsible for each step
- what decisions are made
- what exceptions happen
- what improvements may be needed

After gathering this information, the analyst can synthesize the ideas and begin creating the process map.

---

# Why Stakeholders Matter in Process Mapping

Stakeholders often know the real process better than documents do.

Sometimes the official process and the actual process are different.

Talking to stakeholders helps the analyst understand:

- what is supposed to happen
- what actually happens
- where confusion exists
- where delays or bottlenecks occur
