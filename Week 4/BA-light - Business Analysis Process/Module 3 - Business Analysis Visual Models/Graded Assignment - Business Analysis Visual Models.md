### True or false? Creating visuals of business processes puts an analyst in a better position to understand how those aspects of the business function so that strengths and weaknesses can be more effectively analyzed.
- True
> This is the main reason that visuals should be included in business analysis documentation.

---

### Creating a visual model of a business process can help clarify who does what during the process. This use for visual modeling is known as what?
- Role visualization
> By mapping a business process and identifying who is in charge of which steps of the process, it becomes easy to see who is in which role, as well as where there may be steps for which no one is ultimately responsible.

### In a process flowchart, which of the following symbols is used to indicate a decision that must be made?
- Diamond
> A diamond is used to indicate a decision that must be made. Depending on the answer (“yes” or “no”), a specific process will begin.

---

### In a process flowchart, which of the following symbols is used to describe the steps of a process?
- Rectangle
> In unified modeling language, a rectangle is used to indicate the steps of a process. Breaking the process down into detailed steps will help to avoid missing potential gaps.

---

### How is a business domain model different from a process flow diagram?
- A process flow diagram illustrates a particular business process, showing step by step how that process is carried out, while a business domain model shows how different elements of a business relate to one another without necessarily getting into the specifics of any particular process.
> This accurately describes the difference between a business domain model and a process flow diagram.

---

### In a business domain model, what is the term used to describe the different parts of the business that will be identified in the visual (such as customers, products, and orders)?
- Classes
> In a business domain model, the different parts of the business that you are identifying and visualizing are called classes. In the diagram, classes are housed in boxes.
