# Business Requirements Document (BRD)

## Main Idea

After gathering and analyzing business requirements and creating supporting visual models, the final step in **business requirements preparation** is to document the business requirements.

This documentation is usually written in a formal document called a **Business Requirements Document**, or **BRD**.

---

# What Is Business Requirements Documentation?

**Business requirements documentation** is a clear written record of the project’s problem, goals, scope, requirements, and success outcomes.

It helps make sure everyone involved in the project understands:

- what problem is being solved
- what the project is trying to achieve
- what requirements must be met
- what outcomes define success
- what work is included in the project
- what work is outside the project scope

---

# What Is a Business Requirements Document?

A **Business Requirements Document (BRD)** is a formal document shared with stakeholders.

It outlines:

- the scope of the analysis
- the business requirements
- the business problem
- the expected outcomes
- the needs the solution must meet
- the reasoning or data supporting the solution

---

# Purpose of a BRD

The main purpose of a BRD is to clearly define:

- what the analysis project is meant to achieve
- what needs to be done to achieve it
- what business needs must be addressed
- what requirements the solution must satisfy

A BRD acts as a reference point for stakeholders throughout the project.

---

# Why a BRD Is Important

A well-written BRD keeps everyone aligned and helps the project stay organized.

It helps stakeholders make decisions about:

- priorities
- timelines
- project management
- business goals
- scope
- risks
- required work

### Important Lesson

A BRD helps make sure the project stays connected to business objectives and does not move away from the original plan.

---

# Benefits of a Business Requirements Document

| Benefit | Explanation |
|---|---|
| **Clear Communication** | The BRD helps everyone on the project team understand what is expected and what needs to be done by outlining the project’s goals and requirements. |
| **Alignment with Business Goals** | The BRD keeps the organization’s goals as a focus throughout the analysis project by including goal details, metrics, and KPIs. |
| **Scope Definition** | The BRD helps make sure the project team does not move away from the plan by clearly defining the project scope. |
| **Risk Management** | A well-written BRD can identify possible risks so the team is prepared if problems happen. |
| **Documentation** | The BRD is a formal record of the project and its requirements. It should be used as a reference throughout the project lifecycle. |

---

# 1. Clear Communication

A BRD improves communication by making expectations clear.

It explains:

- what the team is working on
- what the project goals are
- what requirements must be met
- what stakeholders should expect

### Why this matters

Without clear communication, people may misunderstand the project or work toward different goals.

---

# 2. Alignment With Business Goals

A BRD helps keep the project connected to the company’s larger goals.

It may include:

- business objectives
- KPIs
- metrics
- success criteria
- goal details

### Why this matters

The project should not only solve a small problem. It should also support the organization’s business goals.

---

# 3. Scope Definition

A BRD defines what is included in the project.

It also helps prevent **scope creep**.

## Scope Creep

**Scope creep** happens when a project slowly expands beyond the original plan.

This can cause:

- delays
- extra costs
- confusion
- missed deadlines
- unclear priorities

### Important Lesson

A clear BRD helps the project team stay focused on the agreed project scope.

---

# 4. Risk Management

A BRD can help identify possible risks before they happen.

Examples of risks may include:

- timeline delays
- missing data
- unclear requirements
- budget limits
- technical issues
- stakeholder disagreements
- resource shortages

### Why this matters

If risks are identified early, the team can prepare for them and create backup plans.

---

# 5. Documentation

A BRD is an official project record.

It documents:

- the business problem
- the requirements
- the project scope
- the goals
- the expected outcomes
- decisions made during planning

### Why this matters

The BRD can be used as a reference throughout the project lifecycle.

---

# BRD as a Touchstone

A BRD acts as a **touchstone**, meaning it is a central reference point for the project.

Stakeholders can return to the BRD when they need to confirm:

- what the project is about
- what requirements were agreed on
- what the goals are
- what success looks like
- whether the project is still on track

---

# What a Good BRD Should Do

A good BRD should:

- be clear and organized
- explain the business problem
- define the project scope
- list business requirements
- connect the project to business goals
- include success measures
- identify risks
- help stakeholders make decisions
- keep the project aligned

---

# Big Lesson

The BRD turns the work of gathering, analyzing, and modeling requirements into a formal document that the whole project team can use.

It helps prevent confusion and keeps the project focused on solving the right business problem.

---

- A BRD helps keep everyone on the same page.

- It defines project scope and helps prevent scope creep.
- It helps identify risks.
- It acts as formal project documentation.

# Extra Important Notes

- A BRD should focus on business needs, not unnecessary details.
- It should be written clearly enough for stakeholders to understand.
- It should connect requirements to business goals, metrics, and KPIs.
- It should help stakeholders make informed decisions.
- A strong BRD reduces confusion, delays, and misalignment.
- A BRD is not just paperwork; it is a tool for project alignment and decision-making.

---

# Final Summary

A Business Requirements Document, or BRD, is a formal document that records the business problem, project scope, requirements, goals, risks, and success outcomes. It helps everyone involved in the project understand what is expected and what needs to be done. A strong BRD improves communication, keeps the project aligned with business goals, defines the scope, supports risk management, and serves as official documentation throughout the project lifecycle. The main lesson is that documenting requirements clearly helps keep the analysis project organized, focused, and aligned with stakeholder and business needs.
