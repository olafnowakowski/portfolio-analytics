### What is the main purpose of a business requirements document (BRD)?
- A BRD clearly defines what the analysis project is intended to achieve and what needs to be done to achieve it.
> A well-written BRD clearly defines what the analysis project is intended to achieve and what needs to be done to achieve it. It provides clarity and focus for the analysis project, helping to maintain organization and keep the project on track.

### Which term is used to describe the situation when a project team begins doing work that is outside the boundaries of the project?
- Scope creep
> Scope creep refers to how the work of a project and a project’s requirements tend to increase or change over time. New opportunities, ideas, and information can come along, and it can be tempting to redefine the project and push it in a new direction.

### Limitations such as budget, resource availability, deadlines, and external regulations would typically be found in which part of the BRD?
- Constraints
> The constraints section is the ideal place to list any limiting factors, which would impact any potential adjustments to the project.

### What is the purpose of including an overall project summary in the BRD? What is typically included in this section?
- The overall project summary section of the BRD provides the framing elements of the project. This is where stakeholders should be able to get a quick overview of the larger project without needing to read the entire document.
- This section provides:
  - the basic vision,
  - goals, and
  - business context of the project,
> including the vision of the analysis and the intended outcome of the project.
