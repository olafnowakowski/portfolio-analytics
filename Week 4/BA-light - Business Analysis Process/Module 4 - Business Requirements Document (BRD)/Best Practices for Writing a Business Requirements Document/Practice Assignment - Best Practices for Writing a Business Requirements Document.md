### What is the main benefit of including visuals in a business requirements document?
- Visuals can help the reader to mentally organize the information within the project.
> A text-heavy BRD can sometimes lose the interest of the reader, and visuals can help the reader mentally organize all the elements of the project.

---

### True or false? In a BRD, requirements should be listed in full sentences.
- True
> While it can be tempting to use bullet points with short phrases when making a list, this can cause confusion down the road. Instead, write out the requirements in full sentences that express the complete thought.

---

### When writing a BRD, using words like "user-friendly," "innovative," "deep dive," or "drill down" would violate which of the following writing best practices for business analysts?
- Avoid using jargon or buzzwords.
> Using jargon or buzzwords can dilute the meaning of the document and potentially lead to confusion, so these should be avoided.

---

### What should an analyst do if stakeholder requirements change during the process?
- Pivot the solution to address the new requirements and remove the outdated requirements.
> The goal of the BRD is to address stakeholder requirements, so if those change, the project may need to pivot as well.

---

### What is wrong with the following requirement, as it is written? Improve the user experience of our e-commerce website.
- It lacks specificity.
> While it is technically a requirement, it doesn’t provide any guidance to the project team about how that experience should be improved or how the team will know if or when the improvement has met the stakeholders’ expectations.
