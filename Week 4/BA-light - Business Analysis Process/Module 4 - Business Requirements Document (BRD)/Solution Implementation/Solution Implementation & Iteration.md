# Solution Implementation and Iteration

## Main Idea

After completing the **Business Requirements Document (BRD)** and sharing the analysis with the right stakeholders, the project moves into the next stage:

**solution implementation**

This is where the chosen solution is put into action.

---

# Why Solution Implementation Matters

Solution implementation is often considered one of the most important parts of the business analysis process because this is where the business problem actually starts being addressed.

At this stage, the solution moves from planning and documentation into real execution.

### Important Lesson

A solution only creates business value when it is successfully implemented.

---

# What Happens After the BRD?

Once the BRD is complete and shared with stakeholders, many responsibilities shift from the **Business Analyst** to the **Project Manager**.

The Business Analyst has already helped define:

- the business problem
- the requirements
- the project scope
- the proposed solution
- the success criteria
- the constraints
- the supporting analysis

Now the Project Manager helps make sure the solution is implemented properly.

---

# Role of the Project Manager

During implementation, the **Project Manager** usually takes the lead.

The Project Manager uses the information from the BRD and the Business Analyst’s insights to guide the project execution.

---

## Project Manager Responsibilities

The Project Manager is responsible for:

- managing the project team
- assigning tasks
- setting deadlines
- supporting team members
- monitoring project progress
- making sure the project plan is followed
- checking whether changes are needed
- allocating resources
- managing budget and materials
- helping ensure the solution is implemented efficiently and effectively

---

# Role of the Business Analyst During Implementation

The Business Analyst’s role during implementation can vary depending on the organization and project.

In some cases, the Business Analyst may continue to support the implementation.

In other cases, the Business Analyst may move to another project after handing off the work.

---

## Possible Business Analyst Responsibilities

During implementation, the Business Analyst may help by:

- explaining requirements
- clarifying the BRD
- answering stakeholder questions
- helping the Project Manager understand the proposed solution
- providing guidance on the solution design
- confirming that the implemented solution matches the business requirements

---

# Business Analyst and Project Manager Collaboration

In many organizations, the Business Analyst and Project Manager work closely together.

Their collaboration helps ensure the solution is implemented successfully.

The Business Analyst provides knowledge about:

- requirements
- stakeholder needs
- business goals
- solution reasoning
- analysis findings

The Project Manager provides structure for:

- execution
- resources
- timeline
- monitoring
- project control

### Important Lesson

A smooth handoff between the Business Analyst and Project Manager helps the implementation succeed.

---

# Project Plan

## What Is a Project Plan?

A **project plan** is a document that explains how the project will be executed, monitored, and controlled.

It is usually created or managed by the Project Manager, often with input from the Business Analyst.

---

## What Informs the Project Plan?

The project plan is informed by the BRD.

The BRD explains the business needs and requirements, and the project plan explains how the work will actually be carried out.

---

## What a Project Plan Defines

A project plan may define:

- project scope
- objectives
- deliverables
- timeline
- budget
- resources
- risks
- quality criteria

---

# BRD vs Project Plan

| Document | Main Purpose |
|---|---|
| **BRD** | Defines the business problem, requirements, scope, and expected outcomes |
| **Project Plan** | Defines how the project will be executed, monitored, and controlled |

---

# Why the Transition Matters

The transition from Business Analyst to Project Manager is important because it connects analysis to execution.

If the handoff is unclear, the project team may misunderstand:

- requirements
- priorities
- scope
- risks
- stakeholder needs
- success criteria

Good communication during the transition helps prevent these problems.

---

# Implementation Responsibilities

During implementation, the Project Manager focuses on making sure the solution is properly put into practice.

Important responsibilities include:

---

## 1. Managing the Project Team

The Project Manager assigns tasks and sets deadlines.

They make sure team members understand:

- what they need to do
- when work is due
- how their work supports the solution
- where to get help if needed

---

## 2. Monitoring Project Progress

The Project Manager tracks whether the project plan is being followed.

They monitor:

- progress
- deadlines
- risks
- delays
- changes
- blockers

If something is not going according to plan, the Project Manager helps decide whether adjustments are needed.

---

## 3. Allocating Resources

The Project Manager manages resources such as:

- budget
- materials
- tools
- people
- time

The goal is to make sure resources are used efficiently and effectively.

---

# Solution Iteration

## What Is Solution Iteration?

**Solution iteration** is the process of improving a solution through repeated cycles of implementation and evaluation.

It means the first version of a solution may not be perfect.

The team may need to make changes after seeing how the solution works in practice.

---

# Why Iteration Is Needed

Even when the project plan is strong, changes may still be needed after implementation.

This can happen because of:

- stakeholder feedback
- changing requirements
- unexpected problems
- evaluation results
- new business needs
- technical issues
- user experience problems

---

# How Solution Iteration Works

The solution iteration process usually follows a cycle:

1. Implement the solution.
2. Evaluate how well it works.
3. Gather feedback or data.
4. Identify what needs to change.
5. Improve the solution.
6. Test or evaluate again.
7. Repeat until the solution successfully addresses the problem.

---

# Iteration May Happen Many Times

The solution may go through several rounds of changes before the final version fully solves the business problem.

This is normal.

### Important Lesson

Implementation is not always a one-time event. A solution may need refinement before it creates the intended value.

---

# Evaluating the Value of the Solution

After implementation, the team needs to evaluate whether the solution actually worked.

This evaluation should be based on the success criteria and business objectives defined earlier.

The next lesson focuses on evaluating value created by the solution.

---

# Big Lesson

Solution implementation turns the work from the BRD into real action.

The Business Analyst helps define the problem and requirements, while the Project Manager usually leads the implementation.

After implementation, the solution may need to be improved through iteration.

---

# Key Takeaways

- Solution implementation happens after the BRD is completed and shared with stakeholders.
- Implementation is where the chosen solution is brought to life.
- The Project Manager usually takes the lead during implementation.
- The Business Analyst may still provide guidance, clarification, and support.
- The BRD informs the project plan.
- A project plan explains how the project will be executed, monitored, and controlled.
- A project plan may include scope, objectives, deliverables, timeline, budget, resources, risks, and quality criteria.
- Good communication between the Business Analyst and Project Manager supports a smooth handoff.
- The Project Manager manages tasks, deadlines, resources, and progress.
- Solution iteration means improving the solution through repeated cycles of implementation and evaluation.
- Feedback, requirement changes, or evaluation results may lead to changes in the solution.
- A solution may need several iterations before it fully solves the problem.
- The final goal is to make sure the solution meets the business needs and requirements outlined in the BRD.

---

# Extra Important Notes

- A BRD does not solve the problem by itself; it guides the solution.
- Implementation connects analysis to business value.
- The Project Manager ensures the solution is delivered effectively.
- The Business Analyst helps protect the connection between the solution and the original requirements.
- Iteration is not failure; it is part of improving the solution.
- Evaluation is needed to confirm whether the solution achieved the intended goals.
- Implementation should stay aligned with the BRD, project scope, and business objectives.

---

# Final Summary

After the BRD is completed and shared, the project moves into solution implementation. During this phase, responsibility often shifts from the Business Analyst to the Project Manager. The Project Manager uses the BRD to create or guide a project plan, manage the team, assign tasks, monitor progress, allocate resources, and ensure the solution is implemented properly. The Business Analyst may still help by clarifying requirements and supporting the handoff. After implementation, the solution may need to be refined through solution iteration, which means making improvements based on feedback, changing requirements, or evaluation results. The main lesson is that implementation brings the solution to life, while iteration helps make sure the final solution truly meets the business need.
