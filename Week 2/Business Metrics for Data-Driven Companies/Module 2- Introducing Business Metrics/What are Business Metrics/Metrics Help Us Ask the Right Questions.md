# Asking the Right Business Question

## Main Idea
The highest and best use of a **business analyst** or **business data analyst** is not just to collect data or create reports.

The most important job is to:

1. find the **right question to ask**
2. find the best possible answer using the available time and resources
3. turn that answer into a practical recommendation
4. communicate the recommendation clearly to the right decision-makers

---

# Best Use of a Business Analyst

## What a business analyst should do
A business analyst should focus on finding the right question and then finding the best possible answer.

But the answer must also have practical impact.

That means the analyst should translate the answer into:

- a specific call to action
- a recommended business action
- a clear message for decision-makers

## How the answer should be communicated
The recommendation should be communicated using:

- clear visuals
- simple metaphors
- non-technical language

### Important lesson
The goal is not just to be technically correct.  
The goal is to help decision-makers understand what to do next.

---

# The Most Important Business Question

The speaker says the right question is usually:

> What change in our business processes can and should we make right now to increase revenues, maximize profitability, or reduce risk?

This question is powerful because it focuses on action.

It asks:

- what should change?
- can the change happen now?
- how will it help the business?
- will it increase revenue, improve profit, or reduce risk?

---

# What Does “Right Now” Mean?

The phrase **right now** can mean different things depending on the company’s systems and capabilities.

The speaker gives three levels of “right now.”

---

## 1. Best Case: Real-Time Computerized Systems

The ideal situation is when a company has real-time computerized systems that can automatically respond to customer and market data.

These systems can:

- optimize dynamic metrics
- respond to customer behavior
- respond to market input
- make changes almost instantly

### Example
A system may adjust recommendations, prices, or responses based on live customer activity.

### Timing
The delay is extremely small, usually only a fraction of a second.

This delay is called **latency**.

---

## 2. Next Best Case: Just-in-Time Human Response

If a fully automated response is not possible, the next best option is a **just-in-time** human response.

This means a person responds to the customer, but they have access to the full customer history.

The employee can see records such as:

- past purchases
- customer support calls
- website actions
- previous interactions with the company

### Why this matters
The human employee can make a better decision because they understand the customer’s full relationship with the company.

---

## 3. Third Best Case: Tested Change Through A/B Testing

Sometimes a recommended change cannot happen immediately.

But it can happen after it has been tested and proven to work.

This is where **A/B testing** is useful.

---

# What Is A/B Testing?

**A/B testing** means comparing two versions of something to see which performs better.

For example, a company might create:

- Version A of a webpage
- Version B of a webpage

Then the company compares which version produces better results.

## Why A/B testing is useful
A/B testing helps businesses make decisions based on evidence instead of guessing.

## Timing
The speaker says an A/B test can often begin within **30 days** of a recommendation.

The recommended change can then be made once enough data has been collected to show that one option is clearly better than the other.

---

# What Are Metrics?

Metrics are special numbers that help analysts ask and answer important business questions.

They help answer questions like:

- What decision should we make?
- What process should we change right now?
- What can we improve?
- What result are we trying to influence?

## Important definition
Metrics are numbers that can be impacted when a business changes its processes.

### Simple meaning
A number is only a useful business metric if the business can actually change it through action.

---

# What Is Not a Metric?

The speaker gives an example of a local retail clothing store in North Carolina.

In North Carolina, the sales tax is **4.75%**.

For that store, the sales tax is **not a metric** because the business cannot change it.

It is more like:

- a fixed rule
- a fact of the environment
- something outside the company’s control

### Important lesson
If the business cannot influence the number, it is not a useful metric for process improvement.

---

# What Is a Good Metric?

A good metric is something the business can change through its decisions.

## Example: Advertisement Click-Through
The percentage of people who:

1. view an advertisement on a website
2. click the ad
3. go to the company homepage

is a useful metric.

Why?

Because the business can change this number by changing:

- the ad content
- the ad appearance
- the time of day the ad runs
- the websites where the ad appears
- the audience targeted by the ad

### Important lesson
A metric should be connected to something the business can adjust.

---

# Big Lesson
A business analyst should focus on actionable questions and actionable metrics.

The best analysis leads to a clear recommendation about what the business should do next.

Good analysis should help the company:

- increase revenue
- maximize profitability
- reduce risk
- improve business processes

---

# Key Takeaways
- The best use of a business analyst is to find the right question to ask.
- After finding the right question, the analyst should find the best possible answer with available time and resources.
- The answer should become a clear recommended action.
- Recommendations should be communicated to decision-makers using simple, non-technical language.
- The most important business question is usually about what process change should be made now.
- “Right now” can mean real-time automated response, just-in-time human response, or a tested change through A/B testing.
- A/B testing compares two versions to find which one performs better.
- Metrics are numbers that help businesses decide what to change.
- A useful metric is something the business can influence through action.
- A fixed number, like sales tax, is not a useful metric because the business cannot change it.
- Ad click-through rate is a useful metric because the business can change ads, timing, placement, and targeting.

---

# Extra Important Notes
- Business analysis is valuable when it leads to action, not just information.
- The right question is usually connected to business processes and business outcomes.
- Metrics should be connected to decisions the company can actually make.
- A number that cannot be changed may still be important background information, but it is not a useful performance metric.
- Clear communication is just as important as analysis because decision-makers need to understand what action to take.

---

# Final Summary
The main role of a business analyst is to find the right question, answer it as well as possible, and turn the answer into a clear business action. The most important question usually asks what business process should change right now to increase revenue, improve profitability, or reduce risk. “Right now” can mean real-time automation, just-in-time human response, or tested change through A/B testing. Metrics help analysts answer these questions, but useful metrics must be numbers the business can actually influence. Strong business analysis leads to practical recommendations that decision-makers can understand and act on.
