# Traditional Enterprise Sales Funnel

## Main Idea
This lesson explains the **traditional enterprise sales funnel** and the most important revenue metrics used to track enterprise sales success.

Enterprise sales are expensive and time-consuming, so companies need to carefully track each stage of the sales process.

---

# Revenue Metrics Reminder

## What revenue metrics are
Revenue metrics are **outward-facing** metrics.

They measure how well a company:

- identifies potential customers
- communicates its offerings
- explains its value proposition
- sells to customers

### Simple meaning
Revenue metrics help measure how well a company is attracting and converting customers.

---

# What Is Enterprise Sales?

## Definition used in this course
In this course, **enterprise sales** means any sale that requires involvement from full-time salespeople in the company.

Traditionally, enterprise sales refers to large sales of complex products or services.

Examples include:

- large capital equipment, such as wind turbines
- construction contracts
- major business-to-business service agreements
- complex products requiring many conversations and meetings

---

## What enterprise sales usually involves
Enterprise sales often require:

- phone calls with potential customers
- relationship-building
- giving the customer time to know and trust you
- traveling to the customer’s location
- in-person meetings
- multiple meetings before a sale is made

### Important lesson
Enterprise sales are not quick or cheap. They require time, people, travel, and relationship-building.

---

# Why Enterprise Sales Must Have a Large Payoff

Enterprise sales are expensive to do.

Because of that, each sale must be large enough to justify the effort.

If a company uses enterprise sales for low-priced or medium-priced items, it may lose money on average for every sale.

In that case, the company may need to:

- change its sales model
- or go out of business

---

## General Rule for Enterprise Sales Size
The speaker gives a general rule based on the cost of operating in the United States.

Enterprise sales may be justified if the sale is at least:

- **$250,000 for a one-time sale**
- or **$100,000 per year for a recurring sale**

### Important note
The exact number depends on the industry and company, but the sale must be large enough to cover the cost of the sales effort.

---

# Example: Electric Delivery Vans

## Product
Imagine your company sells electric delivery vans.

The vans are:

- non-polluting
- low-cost to operate
- limited to a 75-mile radius from the charging station

Each van costs:

- **$50,000**

---

## Bad target customer
It would not make sense to use an enterprise sales campaign on a small business that might buy only one van.

Why?

Because even if the customer buys one van, the sales process may cost more than the profit earned.

---

## Better target customer
A better target would be a company that replaces:

- at least 2 to 3 delivery trucks every year
- ideally many more

### Important lesson
Enterprise sales should focus on customers with enough buying potential to make the sales effort worthwhile.

---

# The Traditional Enterprise Sales Funnel

The sales funnel is the step-by-step process of moving a potential customer toward an actual sale.

The key enterprise sales metrics are:

1. **New leads**
2. **New qualified leads**
3. **Expressions of interest**
4. **Meetings with the correct decision maker**
5. **Soft-circle sales**
6. **Official binding contract sales**

---

# 1. Lead

## What is a lead?
A **lead** is a person whose name and contact information you have.

In the electric van example, a lead is someone who works at a company that owns delivery trucks.

They might buy more delivery vehicles in the future.

### Simple meaning
A lead is a possible future customer.

---

# 2. Qualified Lead

## What it means
The first step is to **qualify the lead**.

A qualified lead is not just anyone who seems interested.  
The sales team must confirm two things.

## A qualified lead should have:

### 1. A plan to buy
The target company plans to buy at least 2 or 3 delivery vans from someone within the next year.

### 2. A budget to buy
The target company can afford vans in your price range.

### Simple meaning
A qualified lead has both:

- a real need
- and the money to buy

---

## Important Warning About Unqualified Leads
Some people may happily talk to you for hours about how much their company wants your product.

But later you may find out:

- they have no budget
- they have no buying authority
- they cannot actually make a purchase decision

They may even invite you to travel and demonstrate your product at your own expense, without telling you that no decision-maker will attend.

### Important lesson
Interest alone is not enough.  
A real qualified lead must connect to a company with both a plan and a budget.

---

# 3. Correct Decision Maker

## What it means
After qualifying the lead, you need to identify the person in the target company who has:

- the budget
- the authority
- the ability to approve the purchase

This person must be able to sign a purchase order.

They are called the **correct decision maker**.

---

## Why this matters
The first person you contact may not be the decision maker.

They may:

- not have authority
- not control the budget
- not influence the actual decision

They may also avoid telling you who the real decision maker is because they enjoy the attention or meetings.

### Important lesson
In enterprise sales, reaching the correct decision maker is critical.

---

# 4. Expression of Interest

## What it means
An **expression of interest** happens when the correct decision maker says they want to discuss the possibility of buying your product.

Example:

> “Yes, we might buy vans from you.”

This means the decision maker is open to engaging with your company.

---

# 5. Meeting with the Correct Decision Maker

## Why this is important
The next major step is to meet directly with the correct decision maker.

This may not be easy, especially in large organizations.

You may have to deal with:

- gatekeepers
- lower-level employees
- requests for documentation
- many intermediate meetings

---

## Warning About Too Many Meetings
Large companies can create endless meetings that seem useful but do not actually move the sale forward.

Some people inside companies have jobs that involve many meetings, so they may keep requesting more meetings even when no decision is being made.

### Important lesson
Great salespeople are good at getting directly to the top decision maker and avoiding endless unnecessary meetings.

---

# 6. Soft-Circle Sale

## What it means
A **soft-circle sale** happens when the correct decision maker clearly says yes.

At this stage:

- objections have been addressed
- concerns have been handled
- terms and price have been negotiated
- the decision maker says the company will buy a certain amount for a certain price

Example:

> “We will buy X number of vans from you for Y dollars.”

---

## Why it is called “soft”
It is called a **soft circle** because the sale is not fully completed yet.

Many things can still go wrong before the official contract is signed.

---

# What Can Go Wrong After a Soft-Circle Sale?

Even after the decision maker says yes, problems can still happen.

Examples from the lesson include:

- the decision maker stops answering calls and emails
- the decision maker quits, is fired, or transfers
- the sales process must restart with a new person
- the target company is acquired
- the target company enters acquisition talks
- pending contracts are suspended
- the target company goes bankrupt
- your own company stops offering the product or service you just sold

### Important lesson
A verbal yes is not the same as a completed sale.

---

# 7. Official Binding Contract Sale

## What it means
The sale is truly complete when there is an official legal contract.

This is when the sale becomes binding.

### Important accounting point
This is also when the company can usually **book the revenue** for financial accounting purposes.

---

# Full Enterprise Sales Funnel

## The key enterprise sales metrics are:

1. **New leads**
   - possible customers with contact information

2. **New qualified leads**
   - leads with both a plan and budget to buy

3. **Expressions of interest**
   - correct decision makers show interest in buying

4. **Meetings with the correct decision maker**
   - direct meetings with the person who can approve the purchase

5. **Soft-circle sales**
   - decision maker says yes, but no final contract yet

6. **Official binding contract sales**
   - legal contract is signed and revenue can be booked

---

# Big Lesson
Enterprise sales success depends on tracking each stage of the funnel.

It is not enough to know that people are interested.

A company must know:

- how many leads it has
- how many are qualified
- how many decision makers are interested
- how many meetings happen with real decision makers
- how many soft yeses happen
- how many become binding contracts

---

# Key Takeaways
- Revenue metrics measure how well a company identifies, communicates with, and sells to customers.
- Enterprise sales require full-time salespeople and often involve complex, expensive sales processes.
- Enterprise sales usually include phone calls, travel, meetings, relationship-building, and negotiation.
- Because enterprise sales are expensive, the sale must be large enough to justify the effort.
- A general rule is at least $250,000 for a one-time sale or $100,000 per year for a recurring sale.
- A lead is a possible future customer.
- A qualified lead has both a plan to buy and a budget to buy.
- The correct decision maker is the person with authority and budget to approve the purchase.
- An expression of interest happens when the decision maker says they may want to buy.
- A soft-circle sale happens when the decision maker says yes, but before the legal contract is signed.
- Many things can still go wrong after a soft-circle sale.
- A completed sale requires an official binding contract.
- Revenue is booked when the official contract sale is completed.

---

# Extra Important Notes
- Enterprise sales should not be used for small purchases because the sales cost can exceed the profit.
- Talking to friendly people is not the same as selling to qualified buyers.
- Budget and authority matter as much as interest.
- The faster a salesperson reaches the correct decision maker, the more efficient the sales process becomes.
- The sales funnel helps a company track progress from early interest to real revenue.
- A “yes” is not final until the contract is signed.

---

# Final Summary
Traditional enterprise sales involve selling large or complex products and services through a process that requires full-time salespeople, relationship-building, meetings, and negotiation. Because enterprise sales are expensive, companies must target customers with enough buying potential to justify the effort. The enterprise sales funnel begins with leads, then qualified leads, expressions of interest, meetings with the correct decision maker, soft-circle sales, and finally official binding contract sales. Tracking each stage is important because many potential sales never become real revenue. The main goal is to move qualified customers through the funnel until a legal contract is signed and the revenue can be booked.
