# Case Study: How a Profitable Growing Company Can Go Bankrupt

## Main Idea
This case study shows how a company can be **profitable on paper** but still go bankrupt because it runs out of cash.

The example uses **Egger’s Roast Coffee**, a coffee roasting business that sells wholesale coffee to a grocery chain.

The key lesson is:

> Profitability and cash flow are not the same thing.

A company can make a profit on every sale but still fail if it does not have enough cash available when bills are due.

---

# Scenario 1: Steady Sales

## Business Setup
Egger’s Roast Coffee sells:

- **25,000 pounds of coffee per month**
- at **$6 per pound**
- with an average total cost of **$5 per pound**

This creates:

- **$1 profit per pound**

So, at first, everything looks good.
