### In business analysis, why is it important to identify stakeholders?
- to understand who will be affected by and can influence the project
> A stakeholder is any person who might impact or be impacted by the project. Identifying stakeholders helps understand who is affected by and who can influence the project.

### What are some of the benefits to proactively managing relationships with stakeholders?
- Identify the interests and motivations of stakeholders. This increases the chances that stakeholders remain satisfied and aligned.
- Anticipate conflicts and risks.
- Gain champions who can help remove these challenges if the need arises.
- Uncover new resources.

### Imagine a new football field is being built at a school. One of the stakeholders, the football coach, wants the football field built. However, the coach doesn't have the authority to make decisions about the project. If you were to map this stakeholder's characteristics on a power/interest grid, where would the coach fall?
- High Interest, Low Power
> As the coach of the team that would play on the new field, this stakeholder likely has fairly high interest. However, because the coach doesn’t have the authority to make decisions about the project, their power is fairly low.

### In the power/interest grid, stakeholders with high interest, low power should be ___________.
 - kept informed
> The power/interest grid is a data representation tool that helps you understand how important stakeholders are to a successful project outcome and how closely you should engage with each stakeholder. A stakeholder with high interest, low power should be kept informed of updates to the project, and their opinions need to be acknowledged, but their ability to impact the project outcome is low.

### True or false? The purpose of a stakeholder analysis is to identify, analyze, and classify the stakeholders in a project.
- True
> A stakeholder analysis is a technique used to identify, analyze, and classify the stakeholders in a project.
