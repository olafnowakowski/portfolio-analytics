# Problem Statement Framework

## Question to be Resolved
How can the grocery chain reduce market share loss and improve its competitive position against the new store entering the marketplace?

## Context
- The grocery chain has recently been losing market share to a new competitor.
- The new store may be attracting customers through better pricing, stronger promotions, a better shopping experience, or a product mix that customers prefer.
- This decline in market share could also lead to lower revenue, reduced customer loyalty, and weaker long-term profitability.
- The company needs to better understand why customers are shifting to the competitor before making business changes.

## Key Stakeholders
- Chief Executive Officer
- Chief Operations Officer
- Chief Marketing Officer
- Store Managers
- Regional Managers
- Sales and Marketing Teams
- Finance Team
- Customers

## Success Criteria
- Increase customer retention by **10% within 6 months**.
- Recover at least **5% of lost market share within 12 months**.
- Increase monthly store revenue by **8% within 9 months**.
- Improve customer satisfaction scores by **15% within 6 months**.

## Constraints
- Limited budget for promotions, pricing changes, or store improvements.
- Strong competition from the new store in the same market area.
- Time pressure to respond before customer loss becomes permanent.
- Possible supply chain or staffing limitations that may affect service and product availability.
- Existing contracts or pricing agreements that may limit how quickly the company can adjust.

---

## Example Problem Statement
The grocery chain is losing market share to a newly opened competitor in the same marketplace. This issue may be connected to pricing, customer experience, promotions, product selection, or other operational factors. The purpose of this analysis is to identify the key reasons for the decline and define measurable ways to recover market share, improve customer retention, and strengthen overall business performance within current budget and operational limits.
