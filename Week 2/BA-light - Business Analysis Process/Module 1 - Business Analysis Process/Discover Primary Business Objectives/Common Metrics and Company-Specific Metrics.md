# Common KPIs and Business Metrics

## Main Idea
Metrics can be almost anything a business measures, but **KPIs (Key Performance Indicators)** are the most important metrics because they are tied directly to business goals.

Different industries use different KPIs, but the main purpose is the same:

- measure business performance
- track progress toward goals
- help teams make better decisions
- identify problems and opportunities

---

## KPI Reminder
A **KPI** is a metric connected to a specific goal.

### Important idea
- **All KPIs are metrics**
- **Not all metrics are KPIs**

Businesses usually focus on a smaller set of KPIs instead of tracking every possible metric.

# Common KPIs Used in Many Businesses

The reading highlights several KPIs that are common across many companies.

---

## 1. Sales Revenue

### What it is
**Sales revenue** is the income a business earns from purchases.

### Why it matters
This is often one of the first KPIs a company monitors because the business depends heavily on sales to survive.

### Why analysts look deeper
High-level sales data gives a general view of business health, but it is also useful to break it down by:

- product
- geography
- demographics
- season
- location

This helps reveal what is actually driving performance.

---

## 2. Customer Retention

### What it is
**Customer retention** measures how well a company keeps its customers over time.

### Why it matters
Customer loyalty is closely related to retention.

A company improves retention by:

- attracting the right customers
- offering products or services that solve their problems
- encouraging repeat purchases
- creating experiences that lead customers to recommend the business to others

---

## 3. Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

### What it is
**Customer acquisition cost (CAC)** compares the cost of gaining customers to the sales those customers generate.

It is often used to evaluate whether a company’s marketing efforts are worth the cost.

### Why it matters
CAC helps a company understand whether it should change or improve its marketing strategy.

---

## 4. Revenue by Month and Quarter

### What it is
This tracks revenue over time on a monthly and quarterly basis.

### Why it matters
Tracking revenue this way can help a company:

- identify seasonal patterns
- forecast future revenue
- compare performance across time periods
- stay aligned with business goals

# You Do Not Need to Memorize Every Metric

The reading makes an important point:

You do **not** need to memorize every business metric or formula.

Most of the time, you will use:

- common industry metrics
- already-established KPIs

As you gain experience, you will get better at recognizing:

- which metrics are useful
- which metrics matter most for the project
- when a custom metric may be needed

### Important lesson
Sometimes analysts create their own metrics for:

- unique situations
- company-specific objectives
- special business problems

# When Using Company-Specific Metrics

If you work with custom or company-specific metrics, you need to understand:

- what the metric actually measures
- what success looks like for that metric
- what failure looks like for that metric

### Important lesson
These definitions can be different for every company.

A number by itself is not enough.  
You need to understand the business meaning behind it.

# What KPIs Help Businesses Do

The reading lists several major ways KPIs support a business.

---

## 1. Monitor Business Health
KPIs help companies keep track of their overall condition, almost like checking vital signs.

### Important idea
A business usually does not need a huge list of indicators.  
A small number of well-chosen KPIs is often more useful.

The reading suggests focusing on a few KPIs in four key areas:

- employees
- customers
- processes
- revenue

---

## 2. Measure Progress Over Time
KPIs help companies measure whether they are moving toward their goals.

Examples of things companies might track over time include:

- revenue
- gross margin
- number of locations
- number of staff

Goals may be set yearly or quarterly, and KPIs can be tracked weekly to measure progress.

---

## 3. Make Adjustments and Stay on Track
KPIs help businesses make changes before problems become too big.

### Leading indicators
The reading mentions **leading indicator KPIs**, which help predict future outcomes.

Examples include:

- weekly new customers
- average sales per customer
- average hours worked in manufacturing
- sales per person

### Why these matter
Leading indicators can warn a business that targets are at risk before it is too late.

---

## 4. Solve Problems and Spot Opportunities
KPI dashboards help businesses quickly identify issues or opportunities.

### Example from the reading
If sales are declining, a company might track KPIs such as:

- sales calls made
- number of new customers
- number of new unique website users

Watching these regularly can help uncover what changes might improve revenue.

---

## 5. Discover Patterns Over Time
Tracking the same KPIs repeatedly can help a business notice important trends.

### Examples
- identifying the slowest quarter of the year
- recognizing seasonal patterns
- discovering that an apparent sales slump is actually part of a normal cycle

### Why this matters
Patterns help businesses:

- forecast more accurately
- plan better
- choose smarter interventions
- understand the bigger business cycle

# Real-World Scenario: Simply Dumplings

## Business problem
Simply Dumplings has been experiencing a decline in profit over the last two years and is currently not profitable.

To understand the full picture, the reading suggests tracking several important business metrics.

---

## 1. Sales Revenue

### Example data
Simply Dumplings generated **$1.2 million** in sales revenue across all locations last year.

### Important insight
When the data is analyzed at both the company and store level, it shows that:

- **4 out of 12 locations**
- generated **60% of total revenue**

### Why this matters
This shows there are major performance differences between locations.

---

## 2. Operating Margin

### What it is
**Operating margin** helps show whether sales are successfully turning into profit.

### Example data
Simply Dumplings has an **operating margin of 8%**.

### Meaning
For every dollar of sales, the company earns about **8 cents in profit** after expenses.

### Why this matters
This shows how much profit the company is making relative to its revenue.

---

## 3. Break-Even Point

### What it is
The **break-even point** is when:

- total revenue = total costs

At that point, the company is neither making money nor losing money.

### Example data
Simply Dumplings needs to make **$100,000 in sales per month** to cover all costs.

### Why this matters
This tells the company the minimum monthly sales needed to be profitable.

---

## 4. Historical Sales

### What it is
**Historical sales** tracks how sales have changed over time at different locations.

### Why it matters
This can help identify:

- long-term trends
- seasonal changes
- top-performing stores
- consistently weak locations

### Example data
The reading shows:

- a consistent **5% year-over-year decline** overall
- but one location had a **10% decline**
- while other stores only had about a **2% decline**

### Important insight
This tells the analyst that not all locations are performing the same, so the company may need location-specific solutions.

# Big Lesson
KPIs and business metrics give analysts a measurable way to understand what is happening in a business.

They help answer questions like:

- Is the business healthy?
- Are we meeting goals?
- Where are the biggest problems?
- What opportunities should we focus on?
- Which areas need deeper investigation?

# Key Takeaways
- Metrics can measure many different business activities.
- KPIs are the most important metrics because they are tied directly to business goals.
- Different industries use different KPIs.
- Common cross-industry KPIs include sales revenue, customer retention, CAC, and revenue by month or quarter.
- KPIs help businesses monitor health, track progress, make adjustments, solve problems, and identify trends.
- Businesses should focus on a smaller number of meaningful KPIs instead of tracking too many.
- Analysts do not need to memorize every metric, but they should learn which ones matter most.
- Company-specific metrics require clear understanding of what success and failure mean.
- In the Simply Dumplings example, useful metrics included sales revenue, operating margin, break-even point, and historical sales.
- Breaking down metrics by store, time period, or category can reveal deeper business insights.

---

# Extra Important Notes
- A KPI is only useful if it is clearly tied to a goal and understood in context.
- High-level metrics are helpful, but deeper breakdowns often reveal the real business issue.
- KPI dashboards are powerful because they make important measures visible and easier to monitor regularly.
- One metric alone rarely tells the full story. Analysts need to compare, segment, and track over time.
- Good KPI selection helps build a more accountable and data-driven company culture.

KPIs are the most important metrics a business tracks because they are directly connected to company goals.
