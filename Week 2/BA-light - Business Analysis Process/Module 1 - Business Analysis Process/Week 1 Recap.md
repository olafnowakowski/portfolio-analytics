# Key Takeaways: Business Analysis Basics

## Main Idea
Business analysis is a structured way to understand business problems, investigate them, and help create useful solutions.

This section brings together the main ideas about:

- the business analysis process
- metrics and KPIs
- stakeholders
- stakeholder analysis
- problem statements

---

# What Is a Business Analysis Process?

A **business analysis process** is a series of steps used to:

- identify business problems
- investigate those problems
- understand goals and requirements
- develop useful solutions

To do this well, business analysts need to understand:

- the business goals
- the requirements for solving the problem
- the people involved
- how success will be measured

## Main stages of the business analysis process
The process can be broadly divided into 3 stages:

1. **Problem Assessment**
2. **Business Requirements Preparation**
3. **Solution Implementation**

---

# What Are Metrics and KPIs?

## Metrics
**Metrics** are **quantifiable measures** used to track the status of a business or process.

They are measurable values that help show how effective a company’s efforts are.

### Examples of metrics
- number of website visitors per day
- number of ad clicks
- revenue earned on a site
- number of social media views

## Why metrics matter
Without metrics, businesses would have no clear way to know whether they are meeting their objectives.

Metrics help businesses:

- track performance
- measure progress
- evaluate effectiveness
- compare results over time

---

## KPIs
**Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)** are metrics used to measure company performance **in relation to specific goals**.

### Important difference
- **All KPIs are metrics**
- **Not all metrics are KPIs**

A KPI is always tied to a business objective.

### Why KPIs matter
KPIs help a company understand if it is moving toward its goals and whether changes are needed.

# What Role Do Stakeholders Play?

## What is a stakeholder?
A **stakeholder** is **anyone affected by a business problem**.

Stakeholders are important because they are often the people who:

- use the business process
- experience the problem directly
- provide information about how things work
- influence whether the project succeeds

---

## Stakeholder management
**Stakeholder management** means:

- understanding stakeholder interests
- understanding stakeholder motivations
- anticipating potential conflicts
- understanding the environment where the problem exists
- managing communication and relationships effectively

### Why this matters
Good stakeholder management helps analysts get:

- more accurate information
- stronger support
- better feedback
- fewer project conflicts

---

# How Stakeholders Are Identified and Analyzed

Business analysts often use **stakeholder analysis** to identify and classify the people involved in a project.

## Stakeholder analysis
A **stakeholder analysis** is a technique used to:

- identify stakeholders
- analyze them
- classify them based on their role in the project

This usually includes looking at:

- **power**
- **interest**

---

## Power/Interest Grid
A **power/interest grid** is a tool used to classify stakeholders by:

- how much power they have
- how much interest they have in the project

This helps determine:

- who to prioritize
- who to manage closely
- who to keep informed
- who to monitor

### Main stakeholder groups in the grid
- **High power, high interest** → manage closely
- **High interest, low power** → keep informed
- **Low interest, high power** → keep satisfied
- **Low interest, low power** → monitor

# What Is a Business Problem Statement?

A **problem statement** is a hypothesis or educated guess about what is wrong in the current business process.

It helps define:

- the exact problem
- the project scope
- why the project is being done
- the project constraints
- the criteria for success

## Why it matters
A well-defined problem statement helps the team focus on one central question that the business solution needs to answer.

### Important note
A problem statement should describe the problem clearly, but it should **not** propose a solution.

# Key Terms

## Process
A **process** is a group of defined tasks intended to produce a desired outcome.

## Business analysis process
A **business analysis process** is a series of steps for analysis used to identify, investigate, and solve business problems.

## Primary business objectives
**Primary business objectives** are the outcomes you want to accomplish with your analysis.

## Metrics
**Metrics** are quantifiable measures used to track the status of a business or process.

## Key Performance Indicator (KPI)
A **KPI** is a measurement related to a specific business goal that defines a set of values against which metrics are measured.

## Stakeholder
A **stakeholder** is anyone affected by a business problem.

## Internal stakeholders
**Internal stakeholders** are people inside the business, such as:

- employees
- managers
- business owners

## External stakeholders
**External stakeholders** are people or groups outside the business who affect or are affected by the company’s actions, such as:

- customers
- suppliers
- society
- government
- creditors
- shareholders

## Stakeholder analysis
**Stakeholder analysis** is a technique used to identify, analyze, and classify stakeholders in a project, especially by looking at their power and interest.

## Power/interest grid
A **power/interest grid** is a tool used to classify stakeholders by their level of power and interest and guide communication planning.

## Problem statement
A **problem statement** is a hypothesis about what is wrong in the current business process. It explains the problem, gives context, identifies constraints, and defines success criteria.

---

# Final Summary
Business analysis is a structured process for understanding and solving business problems. It begins with problem assessment, moves into gathering and documenting requirements, and ends with implementing and evaluating solutions. Metrics help track performance, while KPIs connect those measurements to business goals. Stakeholders are the people affected by the problem, and managing them well is critical because they provide information, influence, and support. A stakeholder analysis and power/interest grid help identify whom to prioritize. Finally, a clear problem statement helps define the exact issue being investigated, gives the project focus, and sets the boundaries for success.
