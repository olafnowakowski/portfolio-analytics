### How does a process differ from a business task? 
- A process is a series of tasks intended to produce a desired result.
> A process is a group of defined tasks intended to produce a desired outcome. It contains multiple, coordinated steps.

---

### According to the framework presented in this module, what is the correct order of steps for a business analysis process?
- Problem assessment, business requirements preparation, solution implementation.

---

### True or false? When using the business analysis process framework presented in this lesson, it is very important to go through every step in order presented, without repeating or skipping steps.
- False
> The steps in the business analysis process are actually very flexible, and it is not uncommon to complete these steps in different orders or to repeat them many times. 

---

### A business analyst is spending some time analyzing the stakeholders involved with the process that she is investigating. Which part of the business analysis process framework is she in?
- Problem assessment
> An important part of the problem assessment is identifying and analyzing stakeholders.

### What type of metrics are used to measure the performance of a company in relation to its goals?
- Key performance indicators
> Key performance indicators (KPIs) are used to measure the performance of a company based on specified goals. 

---

### Imagine you are working as a business analyst for a company working to lower the costs of its marketing campaigns. Which of the following metrics would be best used as a KPI to measure the success of the business solution?
- Customer acquisition cost (CAC)
> Customer acquisition cost examines whether the marketing approach is providing a return on that cost.

---

### True or false? The purpose of a stakeholder analysis is to identify, analyze, and classify the stakeholders in a project.
- True
> A stakeholder analysis is a technique used to identify, analyze, and classify the stakeholders in a project. A power/interest grid is a tool that can be used to conduct stakeholder analysis.

---

### In the power/interest grid, stakeholders with low interest, low power should be ___________.
- monitored
> Stakeholders with low interest, low power are neither interested in the business outcome nor are they heavily involved in it. Their activity should be monitored, as their involvement and influence might change over time. Provide them with relevant updates and monitor them.

---

### In the power/interest grid, stakeholders with high interest, high power should be ___________.
- managed closely
> These are the most important stakeholders, as they are both interested in the outcome of your business analysis process and deeply involved in the business. It is important to build strong relationships with them, consider their opinions, and ensure their support throughout the process.
