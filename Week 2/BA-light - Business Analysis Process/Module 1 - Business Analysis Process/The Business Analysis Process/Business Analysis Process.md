# Topic: Beginning the Business Analysis Process
> Ridez is a ride-sharing service that is located in a few major cities in the United States, including San Francisco, Los Angeles, New York City, and Atlanta. Similar to other ride-sharing models, it relies on freelance drivers who provide their own vehicles. Over the past two quarters, Ridez has been experiencing a downturn in ridership and revenue. Due to the recent decrease in passengers, management is trying to decide if it would be more beneficial to invest in advertising or explore different revenue streams such as food delivery. 
## Question
**What are some possible questions a business analyst may have when beginning the analysis process for Ridez?**

## Answer
A business analyst might ask questions like these at the start of the analysis:

- How much have ridership and revenue decreased over the past two quarters?
- Is the decline happening in all cities, or only in certain locations?
- What is the main goal of this analysis: increasing ridership, increasing revenue, or both?
- Who are the key stakeholders involved in this decision?
- What has already been tried to address the downturn?
- What data is available about riders, trips, revenue, city performance, and customer behavior?
- Are there outside factors affecting demand, such as seasonality, competition, or economic changes?

## Why These Questions Matter
These questions help the business analyst:

- understand the problem clearly
- define the scope of the analysis
- identify stakeholders
- gather useful data
- compare possible solutions
- set success criteria for the project
