# The Business Analysis Process Framework

## Question

## Main Idea
The **Business Analysis Process** is a series of steps used to:

- identify business problems
- investigate those problems
- develop practical solutions

To understand and apply this process more easily, it can be organized into a framework with **three main topics**:

1. **Problem Assessment**
2. **Business Requirements Preparation**
3. **Solution Implementation**

This framework helps create a clear and structured way to approach business analysis.

---

# 1. Problem Assessment

## What it is
**Problem Assessment** is the first part of the business analysis process.

It focuses on:

- understanding the problem
- defining the project
- reducing confusion
- building a clear big-picture view

## Steps in Problem Assessment
This stage includes these steps:

- **Understand the problem and objectives**
- **Identify and analyze the stakeholders**
- **Define the problem statement**

## Why it matters
Problem Assessment is important because it helps remove **ambiguity**.

### Ambiguity means:
- uncertainty
- lack of clarity
- not fully understanding the issue yet

By completing this stage well, you create a strong foundation for the rest of the project.

### Important lesson
Before trying to solve a problem, you need to clearly understand:

- what the problem is
- what the goals are
- who is involved
- what the project is actually trying to accomplish

---

# 2. Business Requirements Preparation

## What it is
After Problem Assessment comes **Business Requirements Preparation**.

This stage is about collecting and analyzing the information needed to support change and build a solution.

The transcript seems to say this stage involves **eliciting and analyzing information**, meaning gathering important details from stakeholders and the business.

## Steps in Business Requirements Preparation
This stage includes:

- **Gather and analyze the business requirements**
- **Create supporting visual models**
- **Document the business requirements**

## Why it matters
This stage is important because it prepares the project for **actionable change**.

It helps turn the understanding of the problem into something more structured and usable.

### Important lesson
You are not just collecting information randomly.  
You are organizing it in a way that helps the business take action.

## Business Requirements Document
At the end of this stage, the goal is to create a **Business Requirements Document**.

This document captures the key requirements clearly so the team can move into solution work.

---

# 3. Solution Implementation

## What it is
Once the Business Requirements Document is ready, the next step is **Solution Implementation**.

This stage focuses on:

- communicating the solution
- iterating as needed
- evaluating the solution’s value

## Steps in Solution Implementation
This stage includes:

- **Communicate the solutions**
- **Implement an iterative solution**
- **Evaluate the value created**

## Why it matters
Solution Implementation is important because it moves the project toward the actual business goals.

It is the part where planning becomes action.

### Important lesson
A solution is not complete just because it was designed.  
It must also be:

- shared clearly
- implemented thoughtfully
- tested and improved
- evaluated to see whether it creates value

# Why This Framework Is Useful

## It gives structure to the analysis process
This framework helps organize business analysis into clear stages.

That makes it easier to:

- understand where you are in the process
- know what to do next
- reduce confusion
- stay focused on the business goal

## It creates a consistent foundation
The lesson explains that some projects may not follow the exact same order every time.

For example:

- some jobs may require steps in a slightly different order
- some steps may need to be repeated

But even when that happens, the three main topics still provide a **consistent foundation** for approaching business analysis.

### Important lesson
The framework is flexible, but the overall structure remains useful.

# Key Takeaways
- The Business Analysis Process is a series of steps used to identify problems, investigate them, and build solutions.
- This process can be viewed through a framework with three main topics:
  - Problem Assessment
  - Business Requirements Preparation
  - Solution Implementation
- Problem Assessment helps remove ambiguity and define the project clearly.
- Business Requirements Preparation gathers, analyzes, models, and documents business needs.
- Solution Implementation focuses on communicating, applying, refining, and evaluating solutions.
- The Business Requirements Document is an important result of the preparation stage.
- Some projects may require repeating or reordering steps.
- Even when the order changes, the framework still provides a strong and consistent approach.

---

# Extra Important Notes
- The process is not just about solving problems quickly. It is about solving them in a structured and effective way.
- Understanding the problem clearly at the beginning helps prevent mistakes later.
- Requirements preparation is what connects problem understanding to real change.
- Implementation should be iterative, which means solutions may need to be adjusted and improved over time.
- Evaluating the value created is important because the goal is not just to implement a solution, but to make sure it actually helps the business.

# Final Summary
The Business Analysis Process is a structured way to identify business problems, investigate them, and create useful solutions. It can be understood through a three-part framework: Problem Assessment, Business Requirements Preparation, and Solution Implementation. Problem Assessment helps clarify the issue and define the project. Business Requirements Preparation gathers and documents the information needed for change. Solution Implementation focuses on communicating, applying, improving, and evaluating the solution. Even though some projects may require different step orders or repeated steps, this framework provides a clear and reliable foundation for business analysis.
