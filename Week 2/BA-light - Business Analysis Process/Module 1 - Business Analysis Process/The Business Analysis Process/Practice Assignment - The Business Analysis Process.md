True or false? A process is a group of defined tasks incorporated into a single step intended to produce a desired outcome. 
- False
> While a job or a task may include just one step, a process includes multiple coordinated steps. 

What are the three main "steps" that organize the business analysis process framework presented in this lesson? Select three.
- Solution implementation
- Problem assessment
- Business requirement preparation

True or false? When using the business analysis process framework presented in this lesson, it is very important to go through every step in the order presented, without repeating or skipping steps.
- False
> The steps in the business analysis process are actually very flexible, and it is not uncommon to complete these steps in different orders or to repeat them many times. 

A business analyst is spending some time creating a process map to help visualize how the various steps of the current process she is investigating interact with one other. Which part of the business analysis process framework is she in?
- Business requirement preparation
> If the analyst is creating a process map, they should have already assessed the problem and moved on to the next main part of the framework. Creating supporting visual models is part of business requirement preparation. 

A business analyst spends some time analyzing the stakeholders involved with the process that she is investigating. Which step of the business analysis process framework is she in?
- Problem assessment
> An important step of the problem assessment is identifying and analyzing stakeholders.

The very last step listed in the business analysis process framework is to evaluate the value created. Explain why this step is so important.
- After the solution is implemented, the analyst needs to check whether it actually solved the business problem.
> Once you have implemented a solution to solve the business problem, it’s very important to assess whether the solution correctly solved the problem. Has your solution fulfilled the business objectives you identified earlier in the analysis process? Does it fill all of the business requirements? Do the costs of the solution outweigh the benefits? Are the business goals now being met? Your answers to these questions may show a need to iterate on your solution or to come up with follow-up projects. If the solution you implemented has not provided the expected value to the company, it’s important look for another solution.
